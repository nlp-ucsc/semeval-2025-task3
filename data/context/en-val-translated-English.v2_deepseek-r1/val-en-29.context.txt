Katsina State is indeed located in northern Nigeria and is predominantly inhabited by the Hausa people, who are the largest ethnic group in the state. The Hausa culture and language are deeply rooted in the region, and they play a significant role in the social, economic, and political life of Katsina State. Additionally, there are other ethnic groups present in the state, although they are in smaller numbers. The Hausa people are known for their rich traditions, including their music, art, and Islamic heritage, which are integral to the cultural fabric of the area.
