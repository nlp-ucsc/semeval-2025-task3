The 1930 International Exposition, also known as the Ibero-American Exposition, was held in Seville, Spain. The pavilions were designed and built by various architects and teams from participating countries. Some notable pavilions include the Spanish Pavilion, designed by architect Aníbal González, which later became the seat of the Archivo de Indias. Other countries, such as Germany and the United States, also constructed pavilions showcasing their cultural and technological advancements. The exposition was a significant event that brought together nations to display their achievements in art, science, and industry.
