Adolf Galland, also known as "Dolfo Galland," was a prominent Argentine regenerated and former military officer. He was a key figure in the irregular militias known as the "Regiment of Mounted Grenadiers" during the civil wars in Argentina in the mid-19th century. Specifically, he participated in the **Platine War** (1851-1852) and the **Paraguayan War** (1864-1870). Galland is often remembered for his role as a vocal advocate for the liberal and Unitarian cause, and his time as a soldier added to his reputation as a colorful and controversial figure in Argentine history.
