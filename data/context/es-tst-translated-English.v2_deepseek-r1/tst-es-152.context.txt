David F. Sandberg is a Swedish filmmaker, best known for his work in the horror genre. Before gaining widespread recognition, Sandberg worked on various short films and projects that showcased his talent and unique storytelling style. One of his most notable early works is the short film "Lights Out," which he wrote and directed. The film gained significant attention online and eventually led to his breakthrough in the film industry.

Sandberg's success with "Lights Out" caught the attention of Hollywood, and he was given the opportunity to direct the feature-length version of the film, which was released in 2016. The movie was a critical and commercial success, establishing him as a prominent figure in the horror genre. Following this, he continued to direct successful horror films such as "Annabelle: Creation" and "Shazam! Fury of the Gods," further solidifying his reputation as a skilled filmmaker. Through his dedication and innovative storytelling, David F. Sandberg has become a notable name in the world of cinema.
