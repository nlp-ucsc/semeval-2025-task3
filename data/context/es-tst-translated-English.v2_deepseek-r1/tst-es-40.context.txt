The director of the first science fiction film "A Trip to the Moon" is Georges Méliès. Released in 1902, it was a groundbreaking and influential work in the early days of cinema. To provide more context, Georges Méliès was a French filmmaker and magician who is often considered one of the earliest and most important pioneers of the science fiction genre in film. His innovative use of special effects and storytelling techniques set the stage for future science fiction cinema. This film, titled "Le Voyage dans la Lune" in French, is celebrated for its imaginative narrative and pioneering visual effects, which were well ahead of its time.
