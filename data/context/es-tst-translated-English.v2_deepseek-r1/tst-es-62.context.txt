The Battle of Svolder was fought between Olaf Trygvesson, the King of Norway, and a coalition of his enemies, which included Svein Forkbeard of Denmark, Eric the Victorious of Sweden, and Olaf Eriksson of Sweden. The battle took place in the year 1000 and resulted in the death of Olaf Trygvesson. This event had significant implications for the political landscape of Scandinavia at the time.
