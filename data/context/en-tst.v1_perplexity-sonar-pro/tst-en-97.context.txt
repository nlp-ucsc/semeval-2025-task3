Yes, Brothers in Arms by Dire Straits won several prestigious awards:

1. Grammy Awards:
   - In 1986, it won the Grammy Award for Best Engineered Album, Non-Classical[1][2].
   - The 20th Anniversary Edition won another Grammy in 2006 for Best Surround Sound Album[1][2][5].

2. Brit Awards:
   - It won Best British Album at the 1987 Brit Awards[1][2][3].

3. Juno Awards:
   - In 1986, it won the Juno Award for Best Selling International Album[1].

The album was also nominated for additional awards:
   - It was nominated for Album of the Year at the 1986 Grammy Awards[1].
   - It was nominated for Best British Album at the 1986 Brit Awards, before winning the category the following year[1].

Brothers in Arms received widespread recognition beyond these awards. It was included in Rolling Stone's list of the "500 Greatest Albums of All Time" and Q magazine ranked it number 51 on its list of the "100 Greatest British Albums Ever"[1]. The album's commercial success was equally impressive, becoming the first album to sell over one million copies in CD format and achieving multi-platinum status in numerous countries[1][5].
