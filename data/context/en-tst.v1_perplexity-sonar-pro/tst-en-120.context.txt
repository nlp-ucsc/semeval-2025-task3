Dioumaténé is located in Mali[1][2]. Specifically, it is a village and rural commune in the Cercle of Kadiolo in the Sikasso Region of southern Mali[1][2]. The commune covers an area of 225 square kilometers and includes 5 villages[1][2]. Dioumaténé serves as the administrative center (chef-lieu) of the commune and is situated approximately 16 km west of Kadiolo[1].

Mali is a landlocked country in West Africa. The Sikasso Region, where Dioumaténé is located, is the southernmost region of Mali[5]. This region borders Côte d'Ivoire to the south and Burkina Faso to the east[3]. The capital city of the Sikasso Region is also called Sikasso, which is the second-largest city in Mali[3][5].
