The deepest known cave on Earth is the Voronya Cave, also known as the Krubera Cave, located in the Arabika Massif of the Western Caucasus in Georgia. As of recent measurements, it has a depth of approximately 2,212 meters (7,257 feet). This cave is renowned for being the first cave to surpass a depth of 2,000 meters and continues to be explored and measured by speleologists who seek to determine its full extent.
