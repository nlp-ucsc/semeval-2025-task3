The Julang-2 is a non-nuclear/cupertino, high-explosive depth charge used by the Chinese military. It is designed to be employed against submarines and other underwater targets. The Julang-2 is typically deployed from surface ships or aircraft and is capable of being guided to its target through a variety of methods. It is considered a significant component of China's anti-submarine warfare (ASW) capabilities, reflecting the country's commitment to maintaining its naval power and security in strategic waterways.
