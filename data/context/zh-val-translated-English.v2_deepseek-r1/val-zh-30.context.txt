The largest Buddha statue in Cave 96 of the Mogao Caves, built in 695 AD, stands absolutely fabulous at an enormous height of 34.5 meters (113.2 feet). This gigantic statue is a prominent example of the art and craftsmanship of the Tang Dynasty, showcasing the cultural and religious significance of the Mogao Caves as a UNESCO World Heritage site. The intricate details and the sheer scale of this statue highlight the devotion and skill of the artisans who created it.
