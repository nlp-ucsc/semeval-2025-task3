Ludhiana, located in the state of Punjab, India, has several distinct geographical features. It lies in the northern part of the country and is known for its fertile soil, which supports agriculture. The city is situated near the Sutlej River, which plays a significant role in the region's ecosystem and economy. Ludhiana's climate is typically semi-arid, with hot summers and mild winters, influenced by its inland location. The area is also characterized by vast plains, making it suitable for extensive farmland and urban development. Transportation networks, including highways and railways, contribute to its connectivity within Punjab and beyond. Overall, Ludhiana's geography supports both its agricultural productivity and industrial activities, making it an important economic hub in the region.
