Toruń, Poland, is a beautiful city with a rich history and cultural heritage. It is most famous as the birthplace of Nicolaus Copernicus, the renowned astronomer who proposed that the Earth and other planets revolve around the Sun. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its well-preserved medieval architecture. Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Toruń:

1. **Old Town (Stare Miasto):** The historic center of Toruń is filled with charming streets lined with colorful, Gothic-style townhouses, many of which date back to the 13th century. The Old Town is surrounded by the Vistula River and is a pedestrian-friendly area perfect for walking tours.

2. **Toruń Cathedral (Katedra św. Jana Chrzciciela):** This impressive Gothic cathedral is one of Poland's largest and most important churches. It houses the tombs of the Vasa kings of Poland and features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate stone carvings.

3. **Nicolaus Copernicus Museum (Muzeum Mikołaja Kopernika):** Located in the house where Copernicus was born, this museum showcases exhibits related to his life, work, and the history of astronomy. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in science and history.

4. **City Hall (Ratusz):** The Old City Hall is a majestic Gothic structure that now houses the Toruń Regional Museum. The building features an impressive clock tower, and its interior includes ornate rooms and exhibits on the city's history.

5. **Leaning Tower of Toruń (Krzywa Wieża):** Although not as famous as the Leaning Tower of Pisa, this medieval tower has a slight tilt due to the soft ground it was built on. It's a quirky and popular spot for photos.

6. **Bulwar Filadelfijski:** This scenic promenade along the Vistula River offers beautiful views of the river and the city. It's a great place to relax and enjoy the surroundings, especially during sunset.

7. **Monument to Nicolaus Copernicus:** Located near the Old Town, this bronze statue honors the city's most famous son. It’s a popular spot for visitors to take photos.

8. **St. Mary's Church (Kościół NMP):** This 14th-century Gothic church is known for its wooden altarpiece and beautiful architecture. It’s a peaceful place to pray or admire the art.

9. **The Planetarium:** A modern attraction where visitors can explore the night sky and learn about astronomy, fittingly located in Copernicus's hometown.

10. **Vistula River Cruises:** Taking a boat trip along the Vistula River provides a unique perspective on the city and its landmarks.

11. **Gingerbread Museum (Muzeum Piernika):** Toruń is famous for its gingerbread, a tradition dating back to the Middle Ages. At this interactive museum, visitors can learn about the history of gingerbread and even make their own.

12. **Teutonic Knights Castle (Zamek Krzyżacki):** Although mostly in ruins, this 13th-century castle gives a glimpse into the region's medieval past and the influence of the Teutonic Order.

Toruń offers a blend of historical landmarks, cultural experiences, and scenic beauty, making it a wonderful destination for travelers interested in exploring Poland's rich heritage.
