Yes, Petra van Staveren won an Olympic gold medal in swimming[1][2]. Specifically, she won the gold medal in the women's 100-meter breaststroke event at the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles[1][2].

Here are some key details about Petra van Staveren's Olympic performance:

1. Event: 100-meter breaststroke (women's)
2. Olympics: 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles
3. Medal: Gold
4. Winning time: 1:09.88, which set a new Olympic record at the time[6]

In addition to her gold medal performance, van Staveren also competed in other events at the 1984 Olympics:

- She finished 10th in the 200-meter breaststroke[1]
- She was part of the Netherlands team that competed in the 4x100-meter medley relay, though they did not advance past the first round[1]

It's worth noting that van Staveren's Olympic success was part of a broader swimming career that included other international achievements. For instance, she won a bronze medal at the 1986 World Championships and a silver medal at the 1983 European Championships, both in relay events[2][4].
