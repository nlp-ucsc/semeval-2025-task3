Trương Tấn Sang served as the President of Vietnam from July 25, 2011 to April 2, 2016[1][2]. 

Some key details about his presidency:

- He was elected as the 8th President of Vietnam by the National Assembly on July 25, 2011, succeeding Nguyễn Minh Triết[1].

- Sang received 97.4% of the vote from the National Assembly when elected[1].

- His term lasted 4 years and 252 days in total[2].

- He stepped down from the presidency on April 2, 2016 and was succeeded by Trần Đại Quang[1][2].

- Prior to becoming president, Sang held other high-ranking positions in the Vietnamese government and Communist Party, including being ranked second in the party's Central Secretariat[1].

During his presidency, some notable events included:

- Meeting with U.S. President Barack Obama in July 2013 to discuss bilateral trade between the two countries[1].

- Working to set a foundation for Vietnam to become an industrialized and modernized country by 2020, as he stated to the National Assembly upon taking office[1].

- Serving alongside Prime Minister Nguyễn Tấn Dũng and Party General Secretary Nguyễn Phú Trọng as one of Vietnam's top leaders during this period[1].
