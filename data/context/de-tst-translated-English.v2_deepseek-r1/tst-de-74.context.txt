The Chinese circle Chengjiang extends over an area in Yunnan Province, in the southwestern part of China. To be more specific, the circle is situated near the city of Chengjiang, which is part of Yuxi City and covers parts of the provinces Yunnan and Sichuan. The Chengjiang circle is well-known for the Chengjiang Fossil Site, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its exceptionally well-preserved Cambrian fossils. The Chengjiang fossils are about 525 million years old and provide significant insights into the early evolution of life on Earth, particularly from the Cambrian period. This area is also remotely located, which contributes to its unique biodiversity and untouched natural beauty.
