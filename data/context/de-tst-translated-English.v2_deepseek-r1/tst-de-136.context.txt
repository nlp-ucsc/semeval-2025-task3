Bahram I was the son of Shapur I, who was a notable king of the Sassanid Empire. Shapur I is well-known for his military campaigns against the Roman Empire, particularly his victories that led to the capture of the Roman Emperor Valerian in 260 AD. Bahram I succeeded his father, taking control of the Sassanid Empire around 273 AD. His reign was marked by efforts to consolidate power and maintain stability within the empire.
