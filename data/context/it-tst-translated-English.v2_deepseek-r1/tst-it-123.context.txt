Air France Flight 296 was a scheduled international passenger flight that crashed on July 26, 1988. The flight was intended to depart from Roissy-Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris, France (CDG) and arrive at Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg Airport in Basel, Switzerland (BSL). However, the flight made an emergency landing in Habsheim, France, after experiencing technical difficulties, but unfortunately, it crashed into a forest near the village of Habsheim, resulting in a significant loss of life.
