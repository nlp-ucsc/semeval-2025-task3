The cloisters of the Convent of the Church of Sant'Isidoro are commonly referred to as the "Chiostro di Sant'Isidoro," which is the Italian term for the cloisters. These cloisters are part of the historic convent associated with the Church of Sant'Isidoro, often known for their architectural beauty and historical significance. They provide a serene and contemplative space, typical of monastic architecture, where monks or nuns could engage in quiet reflection and daily activities. The specific name "Chiostro di Sant'Isidoro" reflects the dedication of the cloisters to Saint Isidore, the patron saint of the church and convent.
