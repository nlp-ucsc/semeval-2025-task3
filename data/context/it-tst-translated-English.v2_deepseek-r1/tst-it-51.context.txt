The scientific name of the brown-headed crow is *Corvus fuscicapillus*. The genus name *Corvus* refers to the group of birds commonly known as crows, while the species name *fuscicapillus* is derived from Latin, where *fuscus* means "dark" or "brown" and *capillus* means "hair," essentially describing the bird's distinctive brown head. This name accurately reflects the species' physical characteristics, making it a fitting designation for this avian species.
