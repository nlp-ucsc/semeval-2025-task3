Petra van Staveren won an Olympic gold medal in swimming, specifically in the 100 meters breaststroke event[1][2][3]. She achieved this victory at the 1984 Summer Olympics held in Los Angeles, United States[1][3].

Some key details about Petra van Staveren's Olympic achievement:

1. Event: 100 meters breaststroke (women's)
2. Year: 1984
3. Location: Los Angeles, United States
4. Medal: Gold
5. Winning time: 1:09.88[1][2]

In addition to her Olympic success, van Staveren also had other notable achievements in swimming:

- She won a bronze medal in the 4x100 meters medley relay at the 1986 World Championships[2].
- At the European Championships in 1983, she earned a silver medal in the 4x100 meters medley relay[3].
- She set an Olympic record with her winning time in the 100 meters breaststroke, which stood until the 1988 Olympics[4].

Petra van Staveren, born on June 2, 1966, in Kampen, Netherlands, represented her country as part of the Dutch national swimming team[1][3]. Her Olympic victory in 1984 remains a significant achievement in Dutch swimming history.
