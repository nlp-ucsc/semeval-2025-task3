Ludhiana is the largest city in the Indian state of Punjab[1][2]. It is the most populous city in Punjab, with an estimated population of 1,618,879 as of the 2011 census[2]. The city covers an area of approximately 310-311 square kilometers (120 sq mi)[1][2].

Some key facts about Ludhiana:

1. Population density: Ludhiana is the most densely populated urban center in Punjab[2].

2. Economic importance: It is a major industrial center of Northern India, often referred to as "India's Manchester" due to its manufacturing prowess[2].

3. Location: The city stands on the old bank of the Sutlej River, which is now about 13 kilometers (8.1 mi) south of its present course[2].

4. Smart City status: The Union Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has placed Ludhiana on the 48th position among the top 100 smart cities in India[2].

5. Business-friendly: According to the World Bank, Ludhiana has been ranked as one of the easiest cities in India for conducting business[2].

6. Education: It is home to Punjab Agricultural University, which is considered Asia's largest agricultural university[1].

While Ludhiana is the largest city, it's worth noting that Chandigarh serves as the capital of Punjab[1]. Other major cities in Punjab include Amritsar (the second-largest city) and Jalandhar (the third-largest city)[5].
