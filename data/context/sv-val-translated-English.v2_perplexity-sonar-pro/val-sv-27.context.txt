Jef Raskin died at the age of 61[1][3][5]. He passed away on February 26, 2005, at his home in Pacifica, California[1][3][4]. Raskin had been diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in December 2004, just a few months before his death[1][2].

Jef Raskin was born on March 9, 1943, in New York City[4][5]. He was a pioneering figure in the field of human-computer interaction and is widely recognized as the father of the Macintosh computer project at Apple[1][2][5]. Raskin joined Apple in 1978 as employee number 31 and initiated the Macintosh project in 1979[4][5].

Throughout his life, Raskin made significant contributions to the development of user-friendly computer interfaces and continued working on innovative projects until his final days. In fact, he was actively involved in completing the code for his Archy project, which aimed to demonstrate a new paradigm for computer use, just days before his death[4].
