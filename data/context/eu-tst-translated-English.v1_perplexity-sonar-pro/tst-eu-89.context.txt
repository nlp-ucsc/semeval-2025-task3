Mercy-le-Haut is located in the Grand Est region of France[1][3]. Specifically, it is a commune in the Meurthe-et-Moselle department in northeastern France[3].

The Grand Est region was formed on January 1, 2016, as part of a reorganization of French administrative regions. It is a merger of three former regions: Alsace, Champagne-Ardenne, and Lorraine[2]. Mercy-le-Haut, being part of the Meurthe-et-Moselle department, was previously in the Lorraine region before this consolidation.

Some key facts about Mercy-le-Haut:

1. It is a small commune with a population of around 280 inhabitants[7].
2. The town covers an area of 13.35 square kilometers[7].
3. Its altitude ranges from 298 to 377 meters above sea level[7].
4. The nearest major city is Nancy, which is about 105 km away[7].
5. Mercy-le-Haut is notable as the birthplace of Albert Lebrun, who served as President of France from 1932 to 1940[1][3].

The Grand Est region where Mercy-le-Haut is located is known for its rich history, diverse landscapes, and cultural heritage. It borders Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany, and Switzerland, making it an important crossroads in Europe.
