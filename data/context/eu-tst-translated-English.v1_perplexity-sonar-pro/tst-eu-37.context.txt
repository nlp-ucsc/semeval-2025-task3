Uta Hagen won three Tony Awards over the course of her career:

1. Best Actress in a Play in 1951 for her role as Georgie Elgin in "The Country Girl" by Clifford Odets[1][3][5].

2. Best Actress in a Play in 1963 for originating the role of Martha in Edward Albee's "Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?"[1][2][3].

3. Special Tony Award for Lifetime Achievement in 1999[1][2][3][6][7].

Hagen's first two Tony Awards were competitive awards for specific performances, while her third was an honorary award recognizing her entire body of work and contributions to theater. In addition to these Tony Awards, Hagen received numerous other accolades throughout her career, including induction into the American Theater Hall of Fame in 1981[3][5].
