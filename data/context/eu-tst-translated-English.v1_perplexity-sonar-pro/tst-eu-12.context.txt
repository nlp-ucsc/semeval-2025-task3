The Boston Celtics play professional basketball. They are a prominent team in the National Basketball Association (NBA), one of the most prestigious professional basketball leagues in the world[1][2]. 

Founded in 1946, the Celtics are one of the NBA's original eight teams and are based in Boston, Massachusetts. They compete in the Eastern Conference's Atlantic Division[1]. The Celtics are widely regarded as one of the most successful franchises in NBA history, having won a record 18 NBA championships[2].

The team plays its home games at TD Garden, an arena they share with the NHL's Boston Bruins[1]. Throughout their history, the Celtics have featured many legendary players and coaches, including Bill Russell, Larry Bird, Bob Cousy, and Red Auerbach, who have contributed to the team's storied legacy in professional basketball[1][2].
