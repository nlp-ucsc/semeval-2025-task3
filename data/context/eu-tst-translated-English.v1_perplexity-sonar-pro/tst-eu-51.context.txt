"Brothers in Arms" is the fifth studio album by the British rock band Dire Straits, released on 17 May 1985[1][4]. This album marked a significant milestone in the band's career and became one of the best-selling albums worldwide. Here are some key details about the album:

1. Release and Recording: The album was released on 17 May 1985 by Vertigo Records internationally and Warner Bros. Records in the United States[1]. It was recorded between November 1984 and March 1985[1].

2. Commercial Success: "Brothers in Arms" was a massive commercial success. It spent 14 non-consecutive weeks at number one on the UK Albums Chart, 9 weeks at number one on the Billboard 200 in the United States, and 34 weeks at number one on the Australian Albums Chart[1].

3. Sales and Certifications: It was the first album to be certified ten-times platinum in the UK and is the eighth-best-selling album in UK chart history. In the United States, it is certified nine-times platinum. Globally, it has sold more than 30 million copies[1].

4. Technological Milestone: "Brothers in Arms" was one of the first albums directed at the CD market and was a full digital recording (DDD). It was the first album to sell one million copies in the CD format and to outsell its LP version[1].

5. Critical Acclaim: The album won several awards, including a Grammy Award in 1986 for Best Engineered Album, Non-Classical and Best British Album at the 1987 Brit Awards. The 20th Anniversary reissue won another Grammy in 2006 for Best Surround Sound Album[1].

6. Track Listing: The album includes hit singles like "Money for Nothing," "Walk of Life," and "So Far Away." The full CD version contains 9 tracks, with some variations in track lengths between the CD, cassette, and vinyl versions[4].

7. Thematic Content: Some songs on the album, particularly "Ride Across the River," "The Man's Too Strong," and the title track "Brothers in Arms," focus on militarism. The title track was written during the 1982 Falklands War and deals with the senselessness of war[1].

"Brothers in Arms" remains a landmark album in rock history, both for its musical content and its role in popularizing the CD format.
