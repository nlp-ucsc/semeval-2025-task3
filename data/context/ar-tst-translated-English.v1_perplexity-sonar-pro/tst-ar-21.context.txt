Dan Schneider was the creator and executive producer of The Amanda Show[1][2][5]. He served as the show's executive producer for 20 episodes from 1999 to 2001[2]. Schneider was also heavily involved in writing for the series, being credited as the creator for 13 episodes and as a writer for multiple episodes[2].

Other key producers of The Amanda Show included:

- Keiren Fisher, who served as line producer and producer for 57 episodes from 1999 to 2002[2]
- Joe Davola, Brian Robbins, and Michael Tollin, who were executive producers for 20 episodes from 1999 to 2001[2]
- Andrew Hill Newman and Robin Weiner, who were co-producers for 20 episodes from 1999 to 2001[2]
- Kevin Kopelow and Heath Seifert, who served as co-executive producers for 13 episodes from 1999 to 2000[2]

The Amanda Show aired on Nickelodeon from 1999 to 2002 and starred Amanda Bynes[1][5]. It was one of Schneider's early successes as a creator and producer for Nickelodeon, helping establish his reputation at the network[3]. However, in recent years, Schneider has faced controversy and allegations regarding his behavior during his time producing shows for Nickelodeon, including The Amanda Show[3][4].
