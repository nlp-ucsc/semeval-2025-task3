Bressana Bottarone is located in the Lombardy region of Italy[1][2]. Specifically, it is a comune (municipality) in the Province of Pavia, situated about 45 km south of Milan and approximately 11 km south of Pavia[1]. Lombardy is one of the 20 administrative regions of Italy, located in the northern part of the country.

Some additional details about Bressana Bottarone:

1. Geographic coordinates: Latitude 45.0789, Longitude 9.13302[2]
2. Population: As of January 1, 2024, the estimated population was 3,454 inhabitants[3]
3. Area: The municipality covers 13.05 km² (5.04 sq mi)[2]
4. Altitude: The average altitude is 68 meters above sea level, with a minimum of 62 m and a maximum of 74 m[2]
5. Climate: Bressana Bottarone has a humid subtropical climate (Köppen climate classification: Cfa)[2]

The town is part of the wider Lombardy region, which is known for its diverse landscape, including the Alps in the north, the Po Valley, and numerous lakes. Lombardy is also one of Italy's most economically significant regions, with Milan as its capital and largest city.
