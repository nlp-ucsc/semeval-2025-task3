Mark Warner belongs to the Democratic Party[1][2][3]. He has been serving as the senior United States Senator from Virginia since 2009[1]. Some key points about Warner's political affiliation and career include:

1. Party membership: Warner is a member of the Democratic Party and has been throughout his political career[1][2].

2. Senate leadership: He currently serves as the vice chair of the Senate Democratic Caucus, indicating his prominent role within the party[1].

3. Gubernatorial tenure: Prior to his Senate career, Warner served as the 69th governor of Virginia from 2002 to 2006, also as a Democrat[1].

4. Senate elections: Warner has won three Senate elections as a Democrat:
   - In 2008, he won his first Senate election with 65% of the vote[1].
   - He was reelected in 2014, narrowly defeating Republican Ed Gillespie[1].
   - In 2020, he won reelection again, defeating Republican nominee Daniel Gade by twelve percentage points[1].

5. Committee roles: As a Democratic senator, Warner serves on several important committees, including his role as chair of the Senate Intelligence Committee[1][2].

6. Political positioning: While firmly a Democrat, Warner has been identified as a radical centrist, often working to foster compromise in the Senate[1]. He has been ranked among the most bipartisan members of the U.S. Senate, indicating his willingness to work across party lines despite his Democratic affiliation[1].
