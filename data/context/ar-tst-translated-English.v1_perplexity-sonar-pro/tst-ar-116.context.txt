The 2011 UEFA Super Cup final was played on August 26, 2011 between Barcelona and FC Porto at the Stade Louis II in Monaco. Barcelona won the match 2-0[1][4].

Key details of the match:

- Barcelona qualified as the 2010-11 UEFA Champions League winners, while Porto qualified as the 2010-11 UEFA Europa League winners[4].

- Goals were scored by Lionel Messi (39th minute) and Cesc Fàbregas (88th minute) for Barcelona[1][4].

- The attendance was 18,048[1][4].

- Andrés Iniesta of Barcelona was named Man of the Match[4].

- The referee was Björn Kuipers from the Netherlands[4].

This victory marked Barcelona's fourth UEFA Super Cup title, cementing their status as one of the most successful clubs in the competition's history[3][8]. It was also Barcelona's second Super Cup win in three years, having previously won in 2009[8].

The match was notable for being a record eighth appearance for Barcelona in the UEFA Super Cup[6]. For Porto, it was their fourth appearance in the competition, having previously won once and finished as runners-up twice[4].
