Chichester is located in West Sussex county, England[1][2][3]. It is the county town (administrative center) of West Sussex[2]. Specifically:

- Chichester is a cathedral city and civil parish in the Chichester district of West Sussex[5].
- It is the only city in West Sussex and serves as the county town[5].
- Chichester district is one of seven districts within West Sussex county[3].
- The city of Chichester lies on the coastal plain of southern England, near the English Channel[2].

Chichester has a long history dating back to Roman times and continues to be an important administrative, ecclesiastical, and market center for the region[2][5]. As the seat of the Church of England Diocese of Chichester, it is home to Chichester Cathedral which dates to the 12th century[5].
