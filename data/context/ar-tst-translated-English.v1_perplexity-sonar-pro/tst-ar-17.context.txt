Douglas County is located in the U.S. state of Colorado[3][4]. Here are some key details about Douglas County, Colorado:

- As of the 2020 Census, the population of Douglas County was 357,978[3][4].

- The county seat is Castle Rock[3][4].

- Douglas County is part of the Denver–Aurora–Lakewood metropolitan statistical area[3].

- It is situated midway between Colorado's two largest cities, Denver and Colorado Springs[3].

- The county was named in honor of U.S. Senator Stephen A. Douglas[3][4].

- Douglas County has the highest median household income of any Colorado county and is ranked seventh nationally in that category[3].

- The county covers a total area of 844 square miles (2,190 km2), of which 841 square miles (2,180 km2) is land and 2.6 square miles (6.7 km2) is water[3].

- Major highways in the county include Interstate 25, U.S. Highway 85, and several state highways[3].

- The county contains parts of three state parks: Castlewood Canyon State Park, Chatfield State Park, and Roxborough State Park[3].

- Douglas County was one of the original 17 counties created in the Colorado Territory on November 1, 1861[3].
