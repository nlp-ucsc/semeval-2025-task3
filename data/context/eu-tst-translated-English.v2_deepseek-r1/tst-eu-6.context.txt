Swahili, also known as Kiswahili, is an official language in several countries in East Africa. It is recognized as an official language in the following nations:

1. Tanzania - Swahili is the national language and is widely used for communication among the diverse ethnic groups.
2. Kenya - Swahili is an official language along with English.
3. Uganda - Swahili is an official language, particularly recognized for its role in regional communication.
4. Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) - Swahili is one of the four official languages.
5. Rwanda - Although the primary official language is Kinyarwanda, Swahili is also recognized as an official language.
6. Burundi - Swahili is an official language used alongside Kirundi and French.

Additionally, Swahili is a lingua franca in other parts of East and Central Africa, meaning it is widely understood and used as a common means of communication across different regions, even if it is not always an official language in every country.
