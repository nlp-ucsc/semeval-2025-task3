Teri Hatcher plays Susan Delfino (n√©e Bremmer, formerly Mayer) in the ABC television series Desperate Housewives[1][2]. Hatcher portrayed Susan for the entire run of the show, from its pilot episode on October 3, 2004, to the series finale on May 13, 2012[1].

Susan Delfino is one of the four main characters on Desperate Housewives. She is characterized as a clumsy, romantic woman with a magnetic charm[1]. Her storylines often revolve around her romantic relationships, particularly with Mike Delfino (played by James Denton), whom she marries twice during the series[1].

Teri Hatcher's performance as Susan earned her critical acclaim and several awards, including:

1. A Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Musical or Comedy in 2005[1][6]
2. A Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Comedy Series in 2005[6]
3. Three Screen Actors Guild Awards as part of the ensemble cast (2005, 2006, 2007)[6]

Hatcher's portrayal of Susan is known for its comedic timing and vulnerability[1]. The role significantly revived Hatcher's career, making her one of America's favorite "Desperate Housewives"[6].

Before being cast as Susan, Hatcher was already known for her role as Lois Lane in the 1990s series Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman[6]. Her audition for Desperate Housewives in January 2004 impressed series creator Marc Cherry immensely, with Cherry describing it as "the best audition I've ever seen in network"[1].
