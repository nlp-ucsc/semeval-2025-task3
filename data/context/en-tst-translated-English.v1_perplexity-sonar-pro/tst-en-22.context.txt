Mario Bolatti played for the Argentina national football team[1][2]. He represented Argentina in 12 FIFA matches and scored 1 goal for his country[2]. 

Some key details about Bolatti's international career:

1. He made his debut for Argentina on August 12, 2009 in a friendly match against Russia[1][2].

2. Bolatti scored his only international goal on October 14, 2009 in a crucial World Cup qualifier against Uruguay. This goal in the 84th minute secured Argentina's qualification for the 2010 FIFA World Cup[3].

3. He was included in Argentina's 23-man squad for the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa by manager Diego Maradona[3].

4. Bolatti played in two World Cup 2010 Group B matches - against South Korea on June 17, 2010 and against Greece on June 22, 2010[2].

5. His last appearance for Argentina came on September 28, 2011 in a friendly match[2].

Throughout his international career from 2009 to 2011, Bolatti made a total of 12 appearances for Argentina, including 5 starts and 7 substitute appearances[2]. While he wasn't a regular starter, his crucial goal in World Cup qualification highlighted his importance to the team during that period.
