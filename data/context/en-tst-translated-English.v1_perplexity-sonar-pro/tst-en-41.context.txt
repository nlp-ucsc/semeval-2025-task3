Chassagnes is located in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of south-east central France[3]. More specifically, it is a commune in the Haute-Loire department of this region[1][3].

Some key details about Chassagnes:

1. Location: It is situated near the national 102 road and the town of Paulhaguet[1].

2. Geography: 
   - The commune's altitude ranges from 529m to 871m above sea level[1].
   - It has a surface area of 12.24 km²[1].

3. Demographics:
   - The population is approximately 156 inhabitants[1].
   - This gives it a population density of about 12 inhabitants per km²[1].

4. Administrative details:
   - Its postcode is 43230[1].
   - The prefecture (administrative center) for the area is Le Puy-en-Velay, which is 47 km away (about 46 minutes by road)[1].
   - Chassagnes is part of the Communauté de Communes (CC) des Rives du Haut Allier, an intercommunal structure[1].

5. Nearby towns: Some of the closest towns include Paulhaguet (2.3 km away), Salzuit (5.1 km), and Couteuges (6 km)[1].

It's important to note that there are other places in France with similar names, such as Chassagne-Montrachet in Burgundy, which is known for its wines[2][4]. However, the Chassagnes referred to in the main search results is the commune in Haute-Loire, Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes.
