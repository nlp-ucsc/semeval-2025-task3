Saint-Voir is located in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of France[1][2]. This commune is situated in the central part of the country, specifically in the department of Allier[1][3]. 

The Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region is a large administrative division in France, formed in 2016 by merging the former regions of Auvergne and Rhône-Alpes. It's known for its diverse landscape, including parts of the Alps, the Massif Central, and the Rhône Valley.

Within this region, Saint-Voir is part of the following administrative divisions:

1. Department: Allier
2. Arrondissement: Moulins
3. Canton: Neuilly-le-Réal[1]

Saint-Voir is a small commune, typical of rural France. It's situated in an area characterized by agriculture, which is the most common land use in the vicinity[2]. The commune is part of the Loire river basin, specifically in the Allier river catchment area[2].

Geographically, Saint-Voir is located approximately:

- 319 km north of the Mediterranean Sea
- 189.9 km west of the French-Swiss border
- 143 km northwest of Lyon Saint-Exupéry Airport, which is the nearest major airport[2]

The terrain around Saint-Voir is hilly, with the hills primarily oriented towards the east[2]. This topography contributes to the picturesque landscape of the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region, making it an interesting area for both residents and visitors interested in exploring the French countryside.
