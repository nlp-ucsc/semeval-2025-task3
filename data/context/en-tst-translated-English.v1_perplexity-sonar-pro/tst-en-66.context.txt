Yes, there is an Indian reservation in Gogebic County, Michigan. The Lac Vieux Desert Indian Reservation is located in the southeastern part of Gogebic County, specifically in Watersmeet Township[1][3]. This reservation is the landbase for the Lac Vieux Desert Band of Lake Superior Chippewa[1].

Some key details about the Lac Vieux Desert Indian Reservation include:

1. Location: It is situated in the western part of Michigan's Upper Peninsula, approximately 45 miles southeast of Ironwood, Michigan[2].

2. Size: According to the 2020 United States Census Bureau data, the reservation has a land area of 0.419 square miles (268.16 acres; 1.09 kmÂ²)[1].

3. Population: As of the 2020 census, 221 people were living on the reservation[1].

4. Tribal affiliation: The reservation is home to the Lac Vieux Desert Band of Lake Superior Chippewa, which is a federally recognized band of the Lake Superior Chippewa[2].

5. History: The Lac Vieux Desert Band was one of the signatories to several important treaties with the United States, including the Treaty of St. Peters of 1837, Treaty of La Pointe of 1842, and Treaty of La Pointe of 1854[2].

6. Federal recognition: The band regained independent federal recognition on September 8, 1988, when President Ronald Reagan signed the "Lac Vieux Desert Band of Lake Superior Chippewa Indians Act"[2].

The presence of this reservation in Gogebic County reflects the long-standing history and continued presence of Native American communities in the region.
