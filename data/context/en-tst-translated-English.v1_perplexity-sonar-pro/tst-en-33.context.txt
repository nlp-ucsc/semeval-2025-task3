Alfred Plé was famous for rowing in the Olympic Games[1][2]. Specifically, he competed in the men's double sculls event at the 1920 Summer Olympics held in Antwerp, Belgium[1][2].

Some key details about Alfred Plé's Olympic career:

1. He won a bronze medal in the men's double sculls event at the 1920 Antwerp Olympics[1][2].

2. Plé competed alongside his rowing partner Gaston Giran[2].

3. Their boat represented the Société Nautique de la Marne club from Joinville-le-Pont, France[2].

4. Plé and Giran reached the final as the third boat without having to go through qualifications[2].

5. In addition to their Olympic success, Plé and Giran were European Champions in rowing in 1920 and runners-up in 1924[2].

After his rowing career, Alfred Plé opened a shipyard in Joinville-le-Pont that remained in operation into the 1980s[2], demonstrating his continued connection to water sports and boating even after retiring from competitive rowing.
