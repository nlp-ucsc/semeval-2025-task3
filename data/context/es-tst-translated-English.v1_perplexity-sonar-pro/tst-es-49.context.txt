To get to Formentera Island, you need to follow these steps:

1. Fly to Ibiza: Since Formentera does not have an airport, you must first fly to Ibiza Airport[1][4][6].

2. Travel to Ibiza Port: From Ibiza Airport, you have several options to reach the ferry terminal:
   - Take a taxi (10-15 minutes, costs around €20)[4]
   - Use the L10 bus service (30 minutes, leaves every 30 minutes in summer)[4]
   - Book a shuttle service or private transfer[3]

3. Take a ferry to Formentera: From Ibiza port, you'll need to take a ferry to La Savina, the only port in Formentera[1][2][3]. 

   - Several ferry companies operate this route, including Baleària, Trasmapi, and Mediterránea Pitiusa[4][6]
   - The journey takes about 30-40 minutes[5][6]
   - Ferries run frequently, especially during summer months
   - A return ticket typically costs around €40-50 per person[4][6]

4. Arrive at La Savina port in Formentera[1][2][3]

Additional information:

- You can also take a direct ferry from Denia on mainland Spain to Formentera, which takes about 3 hours[4].
- During peak season (June to September), there are restrictions on bringing vehicles to Formentera to promote sustainable tourism[1][2].
- Once on Formentera, you can get around by renting a bicycle, scooter, or car, or using public buses[3][5].

Remember to book your ferry tickets in advance, especially during the busy summer season, to ensure availability[6].
