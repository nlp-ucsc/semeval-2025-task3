The 2011 UEFA Super Cup was contested between Barcelona and FC Porto[1][2]. The match took place on August 26, 2011 at the Stade Louis II in Monaco[1][9].

Barcelona qualified for the Super Cup as the winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Champions League, while FC Porto earned their place by winning the 2010-11 UEFA Europa League[2].

Barcelona emerged victorious, winning the match 2-0[1]. The goals were scored by Lionel Messi in the 39th minute and Cesc Fàbregas in the 87th minute[1]. This victory marked Barcelona's fourth UEFA Super Cup title[7].

The match was notable for two red cards given to FC Porto players - Rolando was sent off in the 86th minute, followed by Fredy Guarín in the 90th minute[1].

This was Barcelona's record eighth appearance in the UEFA Super Cup at the time[7], highlighting their consistent success in European competitions. For FC Porto, it was their fourth Super Cup appearance[5].
