Gergely Kulcsár excelled in javelin throw, which is a track and field athletics event[1][3][4]. 

Kulcsár was a highly accomplished Hungarian javelin thrower who competed at the highest levels of international athletics for over a decade. His career highlights include:

1. Olympic medals: Kulcsár won three Olympic medals in javelin throw[1][3][4]:
   - Silver medal at the 1964 Tokyo Olympics
   - Bronze medals at the 1960 Rome Olympics and 1968 Mexico City Olympics

2. Olympic appearances: He participated in four Olympic Games from 1960 to 1972[3].

3. European Championships: Kulcsár won bronze medals at the European Championships in 1958 and 1966[1][3].

4. National achievements: He was a 12-time Hungarian national champion in javelin throw and was the first Hungarian to throw over 80 meters[1][5].

5. Personal best: His personal best throw was 87.06 meters, achieved in 1968[3].

6. Flag bearer: Kulcsár had the honor of being Hungary's flag bearer at three consecutive Olympic Games - 1964, 1968, and 1972[1][3][4].

After retiring from competitive athletics, Kulcsár had a successful coaching career, including guiding Miklós Németh to Olympic gold in javelin throw at the 1976 Montreal Olympics[1][3].
