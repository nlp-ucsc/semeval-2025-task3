Herman Frazier excelled in track and field events at the 1976 Olympic Games in Montreal. Specifically:

1. He won a gold medal as part of the United States 4x400 meter relay team[1][2][4]. Frazier ran the first leg for this gold medal-winning relay team[2][5].

2. Individually, he won a bronze medal in the 400-meter dash event[1][2][4].

In addition to his Olympic success, Frazier also achieved the following:

- He won gold medals in the 4x400 meter relay at the Pan American Games in both 1975 and 1979[2].

- He was an eight-time All-American track performer at Arizona State University[1][3].

- Frazier was captain of Arizona State's 1977 national championship track team[2][4].

- He held world records in the indoor 500 meters and outdoor 4x200 meter relay, as well as the American record in the indoor 400 meters[8].

- Frazier was the 1977 NCAA 400-meter champion[8].

His Olympic achievements, particularly the gold medal in the 4x400 meter relay and bronze in the 400-meter dash at the 1976 Montreal Olympics, were the pinnacle of Frazier's athletic career and launched him into a successful career in athletics administration.
