The fodis are small passerine birds belonging to the genus Foudia in the weaver family Ploceidae[1][2]. Here are some key details about these birds:

## Characteristics

- Size: Fodis are small birds, typically about 5 inches (13 cm) in length[1].
- Weight: They weigh between 14-19 grams (0.49-0.67 oz)[1].
- Appearance: Male fodis are often brightly colored, especially during breeding season. For example, the male red fody (Foudia madagascariensis) is bright red with black markings around each eye[1].
- Sexual dimorphism: Females and non-breeding males are usually more drab in coloration, often olive-brown or greyish-brown[1][2].

## Habitat and Distribution

Fodis are found in various habitats, including:
- Forest clearings
- Grasslands
- Cultivated areas
- Various islands in the Indian Ocean[1]

They are not typically found in dense forest interiors[2].

## Behavior

- Feeding: Fodis primarily feed on seeds, especially grass seeds, and insects. They may also consume fruit, nectar, and other food sources[1].
- Social behavior: Outside of breeding season, fodis are often gregarious, forming flocks[1].
- Breeding: Males establish territories and build nests to attract mates. The nests are typically globular with a side entrance[1].

## Species

There are several species of fodis, including:
- Red fody (Foudia madagascariensis)
- Forest fody (Foudia omissa)
- Comoros fody (Foudia eminentissima)
- Mauritius fody (Foudia rubra)
