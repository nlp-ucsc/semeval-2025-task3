Chichester is a town located in Merrimack County, New Hampshire, United States[1]. Here are some key details about its location:

- It is situated in southern New Hampshire, just east of the state capital Concord[3][5].

- Chichester is approximately 10 minutes away from Concord[5].

- The town covers a total area of 21.3 square miles (55.1 km2), of which 21.2 square miles (54.9 km2) is land and 0.1 square miles (0.3 km2) is water[1].

- It is bordered by several other New Hampshire towns:
  - Loudon to the northwest
  - Pittsfield to the northeast 
  - Epsom to the east
  - Pembroke to the south[1]

- The highest point in Chichester is an unnamed summit at 1,015 feet (309 m) above sea level[1].

- Major routes passing through Chichester include:
  - NH Route 9
  - U.S. Route 4 
  - U.S. Route 202
  - NH Route 28[1]

Chichester is a small, rural New England town with a population of 2,665 as of the 2020 census[1]. It offers a quiet, tranquil setting while still being within easy reach of larger cities and amenities in the region[5].
