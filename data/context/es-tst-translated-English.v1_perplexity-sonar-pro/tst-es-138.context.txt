Kyle Korver plays professional basketball[1][2]. He has had a long and successful career in the National Basketball Association (NBA), spanning 17 seasons[3]. Korver is known primarily as one of the greatest three-point shooters in NBA history[1].

Some key details about Kyle Korver's basketball career:

1. NBA Teams: Korver has played for several NBA teams, including the Philadelphia 76ers, Utah Jazz, Chicago Bulls, Atlanta Hawks, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Milwaukee Bucks[1][2][5].

2. Position: He primarily plays as a shooting guard or small forward[3][4].

3. College: Before his NBA career, Korver played college basketball for the Creighton Bluejays[1][2].

4. Draft: He was selected in the second round (51st overall pick) of the 2003 NBA draft by the New Jersey Nets, but was immediately traded to the Philadelphia 76ers[1][2].

5. Achievements:
   - Named an NBA All-Star in 2015[1]
   - Led the NBA in three-point shooting percentage four times[1]
   - Set an NBA single-season three-point field goal accuracy record in 2009-10, shooting 53.6% from three-point range[1]

6. Career Statistics: Over his 17-season NBA career, Korver averaged 9.7 points, 3.0 rebounds, and 1.7 assists per game[3].

7. Three-Point Shooting: Korver is particularly renowned for his three-point shooting ability. He ranks among the most prolific 3-point shooters in NBA history, sitting eighth all-time in 3-point field goals made and tenth all-time in 3-point field goal percentage[1].

Korver's exceptional shooting skills and longevity in the NBA have made him a respected figure in professional basketball.
