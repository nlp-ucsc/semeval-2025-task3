Laurance Rockefeller served as a founding trustee and leader of the Rockefeller Brothers Fund (RBF) from its inception in 1940 until 1982[2][9]. Specifically:

1. He was a founding trustee of the RBF when it was established in 1940[2][9].

2. He served as president of the RBF from 1958 to 1968[2][9].

3. He then became chairman of the RBF from 1968 to 1980[2][9].

4. His total tenure with the RBF lasted 42 years, from 1940 to 1982[2][9].

5. Laurance Rockefeller's 22-year leadership of the RBF as president and chairman (1958-1980) was longer than any other leader in the Fund's history[2][9].

The Rockefeller Brothers Fund was created in 1940 by the five sons of John D. Rockefeller Jr. - John, Nelson, Laurance, Winthrop, and David - as a vehicle for coordinating their philanthropic efforts[3]. It became one of the primary philanthropic organizations of the Rockefeller family, distinct from the older Rockefeller Foundation[3].
