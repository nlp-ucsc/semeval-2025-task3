La Robine-sur-Galabre is located in the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence department in southeastern France[1]. More specifically, it is situated in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region[6][10].

The commune is part of the Haute Provence geological nature reserve, which is known for its remarkable geological sites[4]. La Robine-sur-Galabre is about 12 km from Digne-les-Bains, the prefecture of the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence department[4].

Some key features of La Robine-sur-Galabre include:

1. Geography: The village is characterized by its black marls and picturesque landscape of wooded and ravine hills[4].

2. Elevation: It is perched at an altitude of 750 meters[4].

3. Population: As of 2017, the commune had a population of 299 inhabitants[1].

4. Notable sites: The commune hosts one of the major sites of the Haute-Provence National Geological Nature Reserve, featuring ichthyosaur fossils[7].

5. Natural landmarks: The highest point in the commune is the Sommet de Nibles (or Petite cloche) at 1909 meters, followed by the Cloche de Barles (or Grande Cloche) at 1887 meters[4].

La Robine-sur-Galabre offers various outdoor activities such as hiking and mountain biking, making it an attractive destination for nature enthusiasts visiting the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region[4].
