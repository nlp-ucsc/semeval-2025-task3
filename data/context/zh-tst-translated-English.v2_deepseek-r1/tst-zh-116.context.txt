"Returning to the original" is a key concept in Chinese thought, particularly emphasized in the expressing of the ideational concept of "Six Classics" of Confucianism and the development of the traditional Chinese mindset. It means to return to the starting point, to retrieve the original state of things, and to seek the fundamental principles. In the context of Chinese traditional culture, it signifies a journey back to the origins of culture, history, and thought, aiming to understand and recover the original, authentic condition of things before they were influenced by external factors. This concept is often associated with the pursuit of truth, authenticity, and the essence of existence.
