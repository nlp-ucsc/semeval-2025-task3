Abrus is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae. These plants are primarily distributed in the tropical and subtropical regions of the world. Species within the Abrus genus can be found in parts of Africa, southern Asia, the Pacific Islands, and northern Australia. Some species, like Abrus precatorius, are known for their adaptability and can be found in various habitats, including tropical forests, savannas, and grasslands. They are often spread through human activity and can become naturalized in new regions.
