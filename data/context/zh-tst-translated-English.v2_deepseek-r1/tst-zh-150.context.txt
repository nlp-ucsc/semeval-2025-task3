Jeff Raskin, one of the original members of the Apple Macintosh team and the creator of the Macintosh user interface, passed away on February 26, 2005, due to complications from pancreatic cancer. He was 61 years old at the time of his death. Raskin was a visionary who played a crucial role in shaping the user-friendly interface of the Macintosh computer, which influenced the development of personal computers. His contributions to the field of human-computer interaction are still celebrated and recognized today.
