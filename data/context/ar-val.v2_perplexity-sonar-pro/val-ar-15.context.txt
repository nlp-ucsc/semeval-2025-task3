Chris Claremont's tenure on "Uncanny X-Men" lasted for an impressive 16 years, from 1975 to 1991[1][3]. This run is widely regarded as one of the most influential and significant periods in comic book history. Here are some key details about Claremont's time on the series:

1. Duration: Claremont wrote "Uncanny X-Men" from issue #94 in 1975 to issue #279 in 1991[1][5].

2. Impact: During his run, Claremont transformed the X-Men from an underperforming title into Marvel's best-selling comic book series[1].

3. Character development: Claremont is credited with developing strong female characters and introducing complex literary themes into superhero narratives[1].

4. Collaborations: He worked with notable artists such as Dave Cockrum, John Byrne, Paul Smith, and John Romita Jr. during his tenure[3].

5. Classic storylines: Claremont wrote many iconic X-Men stories, including "The Dark Phoenix Saga" and "Days of Future Past"[1][3].

6. Character creation: He co-created numerous popular X-Men characters, including Rogue, Psylocke, Kitty Pryde, and Gambit, among many others[1].

7. Sales record: X-Men #1, the 1991 spinoff series that Claremont co-wrote with Jim Lee, remains the best-selling comic book of all time according to Guinness World Records[1].

Claremont's 16-year run on "Uncanny X-Men" is considered a landmark in Marvel history and played a pivotal role in shaping the X-Men franchise as we know it today[3].
