The Cuban men's national volleyball team has achieved numerous accolades in international competitions over the years. Here's a detailed overview of their major awards:

Olympic Games:
- Bronze medal in 1976 Montreal Olympics[1][2][4]

World Championship:
- Silver medals in 1990 and 2010[4]

FIVB World Cup:
- Gold medal in 1989
- Silver medals in 1981, 1991, and 1999
- Bronze medal in 1977[1][2]

FIVB World League:
- Gold medal in 1998
- Silver medals in 1991, 1992, 1994, 1997, and 1999
- Bronze medals in 1995, 2005, and 2012[1][2][4]

World Grand Champions Cup:
- Gold medal in 2001
- Silver medal in 2009
- Bronze medals in 1993 and 1997[2]

FIVB Challenger Cup:
- Gold medal in 2022
- Silver medal in 2019[2]

Pan American Games:
- Gold medals in 1971, 1975, 1979, 1991, and 1999
- Silver medals in 1983, 1987, 2003, 2011, and 2019
- Bronze medals in 1967, 1995, and 2007[1][2]

NORCECA Championship:
The team has been particularly dominant in this regional competition, winning 15 gold medals (1969, 1971, 1975, 1977, 1979, 1981, 1987, 1989, 1991, 1993, 1995, 1997, 2001, 2009, 2011, 2019). They've also won several silver and bronze medals in this event[1][2][4].

Pan-American Cup:
- Gold medals in 2014, 2016, 2019, and 2022
- Bronze medals in 2007, 2017, and 2018[1][2]

America's Cup:
- Gold medals in 2000 and 2008
- Silver medal in 2001
- Bronze medals in 1998, 2005, and 2007[1][2]

Central American and Caribbean Games:
The team has won multiple gold medals in this regional competition, including their most recent gold in 2023[2].

These achievements demonstrate the Cuban men's national volleyball team's consistent performance at the highest levels of international volleyball, particularly their dominance in regional competitions and their ability to compete with the world's best teams in global tournaments.
