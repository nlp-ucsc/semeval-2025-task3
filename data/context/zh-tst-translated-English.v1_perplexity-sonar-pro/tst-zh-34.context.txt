Aconcagua is located in western Argentina, in the Mendoza Province[1][3][4]. Here are the key details about its location:

1. Coordinates: Aconcagua is situated at 32°39'11" South latitude and 69°59' West longitude[1][2][4].

2. Country and Province: The mountain is entirely within Argentina, in the province of Mendoza, specifically in the Department of Las Heras[4].

3. Mountain Range: Aconcagua is part of the Andes mountain range[1][3].

4. Proximity to Borders: 
   - It is located about 15 km (9.3 miles) from Argentina's border with Chile[6].
   - The mountain is about 112 km (70 miles) northwest of the city of Mendoza[1].
   - It's approximately 5 km (3 miles) from San Juan Province[1].

5. Protected Area: Aconcagua is situated within the Aconcagua Provincial Park, which covers an area of 71,000 hectares[4][5].

6. Surrounding Features:
   - To the west and southwest, it is bounded by the Valle de los Horcones Inferior[1][4].
   - To the east and north, it is bounded by the Valle de las Vacas (Valley of Cows)[1][4].

7. Elevation: At 6,961 meters (22,838 ft) above sea level, Aconcagua is the highest peak in both the Western and Southern Hemispheres[1][6].

8. Nearest City: The city of Mendoza, located at 750 meters above sea level, is the nearest major city and serves as the starting point for most expeditions to Aconcagua[5].

Aconcagua's location in Argentina makes it a popular destination for climbers and tourists, offering challenging ascents and beautiful natural surroundings within the Andes mountain range[3][5].
