Mark Warner has held several prominent positions throughout his career in business and politics:

1. U.S. Senator: Warner was first elected to the U.S. Senate in November 2008 and has been re-elected multiple times since then. He currently serves as Virginia's senior Senator[1][2].

2. Governor of Virginia: Warner served as the Governor of Virginia from 2002 to 2006[1][2][4].

3. Business Leader: Before entering public office, Warner spent 20 years as a successful technology executive and business leader in Virginia. He was an early investor in the cellular telephone business and co-founded the company that became Nextel[1][2].

4. Venture Capitalist: Warner was a founding partner of Columbia Capital Corporation and helped create more than 70 telecommunications and information technology companies[4].

5. Committee Memberships: In the Senate, Warner serves on several important committees:
   - Senate Finance Committee
   - Senate Banking Committee
   - Senate Budget Committee
   - Senate Rules Committee
   - Senate Select Committee on Intelligence (where he serves as Vice Chairman)[1]

6. Leadership Roles: Warner has held leadership positions in various organizations:
   - Chairman of the Southern Governors' Association
   - States' co-chairman of the Appalachian Regional Commission
   - Chairman of the Education Commission of the States
   - Past chair of the National Governors Association[4]

Throughout his career, Warner has established himself as a bipartisan leader, working across party lines on issues such as cutting red tape, increasing government performance and accountability, and promoting private sector innovation and job creation[1][2].
