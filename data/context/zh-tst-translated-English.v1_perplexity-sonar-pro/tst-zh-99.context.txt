The 2011 UEFA Super Cup match took place on August 26, 2011 at the Stade Louis II in Monaco[1]. The match featured Barcelona, winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Champions League, against Porto, winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Europa League[1][2].

Barcelona won the match 2-0[1][2]. The goals were scored by:

1. Lionel Messi in the 39th minute
2. Cesc Fàbregas in the 88th minute[7]

Some key details about the match:

- The weather was clear with a temperature of 27°C (81°F) and 67% humidity[1].
- The referee was Björn Kuipers from the Netherlands[1].
- The attendance was 18,048[1].
- Andrés Iniesta of Barcelona was named Man of the Match[1].

This victory marked Barcelona's fourth UEFA Super Cup title, further cementing their status as one of Europe's top clubs under manager Josep Guardiola[7]. It was Barcelona's second Super Cup win in three years, highlighting their dominance in European football during this period[7].

For Barcelona, this match was their record eighth appearance in the UEFA Super Cup[9]. The win added another trophy to their impressive collection under Guardiola's management, demonstrating the team's consistent success in both domestic and European competitions.
