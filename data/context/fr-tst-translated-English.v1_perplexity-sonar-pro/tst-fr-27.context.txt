The genus Foudia includes a total of 8 species[2]. These species are:

1. Red fody or Madagascar fody (Foudia madagascariensis)
2. Comoros fody or red-headed fody (Foudia eminentissima)
3. Aldabra fody (Foudia aldabrana)
4. Forest fody (Foudia omissa)
5. Mauritius fody (Foudia rubra)
6. Seychelles fody (Foudia sechellarum)
7. Rodrigues fody (Foudia flavicans)
8. Réunion fody (†Foudia delloni) - This species is extinct[2].

These fodies are small passerine birds native to the islands of the western Indian Ocean, including Madagascar, the Seychelles, the Comoro Islands, and the Mascarene Islands[2]. They are known for their distinctive breeding plumage, particularly in males, which often features bright red or yellow coloration on the head and sometimes other parts of the body[2].
