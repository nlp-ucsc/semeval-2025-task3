Victoire Tomegah Dogbé is the current Prime Minister of Togo. She was appointed to this position on September 28, 2020, making her the first woman to hold the office of Prime Minister in Togo's history[1][2][3].

Some key points about Prime Minister Tomegah Dogbé:

1. She was born on January 23, 1959, in Togo[3].

2. Prior to becoming Prime Minister, she held several important government positions:
   - Minister of Grassroots Development, Handicrafts, Youth and Youth Employment[3]
   - Cabinet Director of President Faure Gnassingbé[3]

3. Her appointment came after the resignation of the previous government led by Komi Selom Klassou[1].

4. In August 2024, following a constitutional reform that transformed Togo from a presidential system to a parliamentary one, Tomegah Dogbé was reappointed as Prime Minister for a six-year term under the new constitution[1][3].

5. Under the new constitution, her role has been strengthened and renamed "President of the Council of Ministers," making it the most powerful position in the country's government[3].

6. She has a background in economics and management, with a master's degree from the University of Benin-Lomé and additional qualifications from international institutions[6].

7. Before entering politics, she worked for the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in various capacities across several African countries[6].

Tomegah Dogbé's appointment and subsequent reappointment represent significant milestones in Togo's political landscape, particularly in terms of gender representation in high-level government positions.
