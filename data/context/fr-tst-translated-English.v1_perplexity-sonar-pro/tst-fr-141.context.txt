Arden-Arcade is located in Sacramento County, California[1][2]. It is a census-designated place (CDP) situated east of the city of Sacramento and west of the community of Carmichael[1]. As an unincorporated area, Arden-Arcade is not an independent city but rather falls under the governance of Sacramento County for municipal services typically provided by cities[5].

Some key facts about Arden-Arcade's location and governance:

1. It is part of the Sacramento–Arden-Arcade–Roseville Metropolitan Statistical Area[1].

2. The area has a population of 94,659 as of the 2020 census, making it the second most populous census-designated place in California[1].

3. Arden-Arcade's boundaries include Auburn Boulevard and Arcade Creek to the north, the American River to the south, Ethan Way to the west, and Walnut Avenue, Cypress Avenue, and Mission Avenue to the east[1].

4. The community is served by several local government entities within Sacramento County, including special districts for parks, water supply, and fire protection[5].

5. In terms of state representation, Arden-Arcade is in California's 6th Senate District and 8th Assembly District[1].

6. For federal representation, the area is split between the 6th and 7th congressional districts[1].
