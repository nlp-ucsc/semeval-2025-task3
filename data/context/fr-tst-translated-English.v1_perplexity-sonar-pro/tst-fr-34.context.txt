The movie "Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!" has a runtime of 83 minutes (1 hour and 23 minutes)[1][3][6]. This 1965 American exploitation film was directed by Russ Meyer and shot in black and white[1][3]. The film follows three go-go dancers who embark on a spree of kidnapping and murder in the California desert[1]. Despite its modest budget of about $45,000, "Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!" has become widely regarded as an important and influential film in the exploitation genre[1].
