Two Roman emperors were born in Lugdunum, the city now known as Lyon in France:
1. Emperor Claudius (Tiberius Claudius Caesar Augustus Germanicus): Born on August 1, 10 BC, Claudius was the first Roman emperor to be born outside of Italy. He ruled from 41 AD to 54 AD and is known for expanding the Roman Empire and initiating the conquest of Britain.
2. Emperor Caracalla (Marcus Aurelius Severus Antoninus Augustus): Born on April 4, 188 AD, Caracalla was the son of Emperor Septimius Severus. He ruled as co-emperor with his father from 198 AD and became sole emperor after his father's death in 211 AD. Caracalla is noted for issuing the Edict of Caracalla in 212 AD, granting Roman citizenship to all free men within the empire.
