In a case–control study, two groups are compared:
1. Cases: Individuals who have the disease or outcome of interest.
2. Controls: Individuals who do not have the disease or outcome.

The primary goal is to determine if there is an association between the exposure to a certain risk factor and the outcome by comparing the histories of exposure in both groups.

Answer: Two groups—the cases and the controls—are compared in a case–control study.
