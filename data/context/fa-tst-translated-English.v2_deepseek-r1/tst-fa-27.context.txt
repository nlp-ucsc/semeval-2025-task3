The Iranian astronomer who compiled his astronomical tables under the name *Zij al-Khwarizmi* is **Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi**. 

### Key Points:
- **Al-Khwarizmi** was a renowned scholar from the Islamic Golden Age, born around 780 CE in what is now modern Uzbekistan, but he worked in Baghdad under the Abbasid Caliphate.
- The term "*Zij*" refers to a set of astronomical tables used to calculate the positions of celestial bodies, timings of prayers, and other Islamic calendar-related calculations.
- *Zij al-Khwarizmi* was one of his most famous works, laying the foundation for later astronomical tables and contributing significantly to Islamic astronomy and mathematics.
- Al-Khwarizmi is also famous for his contributions to algebra and his role in introducing Arabic numerals (now used worldwide) to the Islamic world.
