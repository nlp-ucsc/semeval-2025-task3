The largest protected area in Iran is the *Kavir National Park*, which covers an area of approximately 4,000 square kilometers (1,544 square miles). It is located in the Semnan Province and is known for its diverse flora and fauna, as well as unique landscapes such as salt lakes, sandy deserts, and dense forests. The park is also home to several endangered species, including the Persian leopard and the Iranian cheetah. Kavir National Park is not only significant for its biodiversity but also for its ecological and cultural importance in Iran.
