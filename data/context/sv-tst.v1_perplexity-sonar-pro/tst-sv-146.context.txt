Alberto Jorge Fouilloux Ahumada played 70 caps (matches) for the Chile national team during his career[1][2]. He was a midfielder and striker who represented Chile in two FIFA World Cups - the 1962 and 1966 tournaments[1]. During his international career spanning from the late 1950s to early 1970s, Fouilloux scored 12 goals for the national team[1][2].

Fouilloux had a long and successful club career as well, playing for several Chilean clubs including Universidad Católica, Huachipato, and Unión Española. He was part of two championship-winning sides with Universidad Católica in 1961 and 1966[1]. Towards the end of his playing career, he also had a stint with Lille in France[1].

After retiring as a player, Fouilloux transitioned into management, serving as head coach for clubs like Huachipato and Colo-Colo[1][2]. He later worked as a football commentator and analyst for Chilean television and radio from 1989 to 2000[1].

Fouilloux's international achievements with Chile included a third-place finish at the 1962 FIFA World Cup, which was hosted by Chile. He also won the Copa del Pacífico twice (in 1965 and 1968) and the Copa Juan Pinto Durán in 1971 with the national team[1].
