Laurance Rockefeller was chairman of the Rockefeller Brothers Fund from 1968 to 1980[1][7]. He served as president of the fund from 1958 to 1968, and then took on the role of chairman for the following 12 years[1]. The Rockefeller Brothers Fund was created in 1940 by the sons of John D. Rockefeller, Jr., including Laurance and his brothers John 3rd, Nelson, Winthrop, and David[6]. During Laurance's tenure as chairman, the fund focused on various initiatives, including supporting civil rights efforts in the American South, providing technical assistance for economic development in West Africa, and addressing environmental and conservation issues[7].
