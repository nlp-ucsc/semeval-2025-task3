Daler Mehndi's song "Tunak Tunak Tun" is famously known from the video game World of Warcraft. The song's dance moves were incorporated as a character animation in World of Warcraft: The Burning Crusade expansion, released in 2007[4][5].

Some key details about this connection:

1. "Tunak Tunak Tun" was originally released in 1998 and became a major hit in India[4].

2. In the 2000s, the song gained international popularity as an internet meme and viral video[4].

3. Responding to this viral popularity, Blizzard Entertainment, the company behind World of Warcraft, added the "Tunak Tunak Tun" dance as a character animation for the Draenei race in the game[1][4].

4. The dance animation in World of Warcraft closely mimics Mehndi's iconic moves from the original music video[1][2].

5. This inclusion introduced Mehndi's music to a new audience of gamers worldwide[5].

6. The song's presence in World of Warcraft has contributed to its enduring popularity and cult status, even decades after its initial release[3].

7. Beyond World of Warcraft, the "Tunak Tunak Tun" dance was also included as an easter egg in another video game, Medal of Honor: Allied Assault Spearhead[4].

This crossover between Indian pop music and a major Western video game franchise demonstrates the global reach and cultural impact of Daler Mehndi's "Tunak Tunak Tun," showcasing how music can transcend cultural and linguistic barriers to become a shared point of enjoyment across diverse audiences.
