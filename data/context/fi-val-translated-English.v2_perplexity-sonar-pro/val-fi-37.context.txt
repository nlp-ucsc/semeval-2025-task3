Nizhny Tagil is located in Sverdlovsk Oblast, Russia[1][4]. More specifically, it is situated in the western part of Sverdlovsk Oblast, in the valley of the Tagil River on the eastern slopes of the Ural Mountains[2]. The city lies 25 kilometers (16 miles) east of the conventional boundary between Europe and Asia[1][4].

Some key geographical details about Nizhny Tagil's location include:

1. It is approximately 150 kilometers (93 miles) northwest of Yekaterinburg, the administrative center of Sverdlovsk Oblast[2].

2. The city spans 22 kilometers (14 miles) from north to south and 21 kilometers (13 miles) from east to west[1].

3. Nizhny Tagil's altitude varies from 170 to 380 meters (560 to 1,250 feet) above sea level[1].

4. The city is built around the extinct volcano Lis'ya Mountain, which serves as a symbol of Nizhny Tagil[1].

5. Another notable geographical feature is Medved-Kamen, a hill located in the northern part of the city at an altitude of 288 meters (945 feet)[1].

6. Rivers and ponds make up about one-third of the city's territory[1].

Nizhny Tagil's location in the Ural region has significantly influenced its development as an important industrial center, particularly in metallurgy and heavy manufacturing[2][4].
