Toruń is currently located in the Kuyavian-Pomeranian Voivodeship (Polish: województwo kujawsko-pomorskie) in north-central Poland[2][7]. This administrative arrangement has been in place since 1999, when Poland underwent a major administrative reform.

Some key points about Toruń's administrative history and current status:

1. Present status: Toruń is one of the two capitals of the Kuyavian-Pomeranian Voivodeship, sharing this role with Bydgoszcz[2][7].

2. Historical changes:
   - From 1975 to 1998, Toruń was the capital of the former Toruń Voivodeship[1][2].
   - From 1921 to 1945, it served as the capital of the Pomeranian Voivodeship[2][7].

3. Metropolitan area: Toruń, together with Bydgoszcz and neighboring counties, forms the Bydgoszcz–Toruń twin city metropolitan area[2][7].

4. Local government: Since 1999, Toruń has been a seat of the self-government of the Kuyavian-Pomeranian Voivodeship[2].

5. Population: As of December 2021, Toruń had a population of 196,935[7].

6. Historical significance: Toruń is one of the oldest cities in Poland, with its medieval Old Town being a UNESCO World Heritage Site[7].

This administrative placement in the Kuyavian-Pomeranian Voivodeship reflects Poland's current regional organization while also recognizing Toruń's historical importance as a regional center.
