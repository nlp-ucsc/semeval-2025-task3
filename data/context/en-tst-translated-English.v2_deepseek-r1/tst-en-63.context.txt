El Al Flight 402 was a scheduled international passenger flight operated by El Al Israel Airlines that crashed on July 30, 1950, near the Swiss-Austrian border. The flight was en route fromLos Angeles, California, to Tel Aviv, Israel, with intermediate stops in New York and London. Tragically, the crash resulted in the deaths of all 58 people on board, making it one of the deadliest aviation accidents at the time. The cause of the crash was attributed to severe weather conditions, including a thunderstorm that led to a loss of control and subsequent impact with the ground. This incident underscored the importance of advanced weather monitoring systems and improved safety measures in aviation, leading to significant changes in flight safety regulations and practices.
