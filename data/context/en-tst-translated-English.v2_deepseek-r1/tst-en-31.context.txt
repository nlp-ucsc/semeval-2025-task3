The Veryovkina Cave, situated in the Arabika Massif within the Gagra Mountain Range of the Western Caucasus in Georgia, holds the distinction of being the deepest known cave on Earth. This natural wonder plunges to an extraordinary depth of approximately 2,212 meters, or about 7,257 feet. This extreme depth makes it a significant subject of interest for speleologists and adventurers alike, showcasing the remarkable geological formations and underground ecosystems that exist beneath our planet's surface.
