Dourbies is indeed a commune located in the Gard department in the Occitanie region of southern France.

The commune of Dourbies is situated in the Gard department, known for its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and diverse natural environments. Gard itself is part of the Occitanie administrative region, which covers a significant portion of southern France. Dourbies, like many of the villages in this area, offers a blend of medieval charm, with narrow cobblestone streets, ancient stone buildings, and a strong sense of community that is characteristic of rural France.

The region around Dourbies is known for its natural beauty, with rolling hills, dense forests, and the possibility of exploring the great outdoors through activities such as hiking, cycling, and horseback riding. The area is also surrounded by vineyards, which contribute to the renowned wines of the Gard department, including therious wines from neighboring regions like the Rhone Valley.

Dourbies, being a smaller commune, may not be as well-known as some of its larger counterparts in Gard, such as Nîmes or Alès, but it holds its own charm and offers visitors a glimpse into authentic rural French life. The local cuisine is another highlight, featuring dishes heavily influenced by Mediterranean ingredients, such as olive oil, fresh vegetables, fragrant herbs, and, of course, an array of cheeses and breads that are staples in French gastronomy.
