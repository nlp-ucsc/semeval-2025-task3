Yes, thePasteur lunar crater is indeed named after the French scientist Louis Pasteur (1822-1895), who made significant contributions to various fields of science and medicine. He is best known for his work on vaccination for rabies and anthrax, the germ theory of disease, and the process of pasteurization. His discoveries had a profound impact on public health and saved countless lives. It is fitting that his name is commemorated on the Moon as a tribute to his groundbreaking work.
