Qayamat Se Qayamat Tak was released on April 29, 1988[1][2][3]. This romantic musical film marked the debut of Aamir Khan and Juhi Chawla as lead actors, as well as Mansoor Khan as director[4]. The movie became a significant milestone in Indian cinema, particularly in the romantic genre.

Some key details about the film's release and impact:

1. It was produced by Nasir Hussain and directed by his son Mansoor Khan[2].

2. The film was a major commercial success, made on a budget of Rs 1 crore and earning Rs 5 crore at the box office[5].

3. Qayamat Se Qayamat Tak received critical acclaim and won several awards, including the National Film Award for Best Popular Film Providing Wholesome Entertainment[4].

4. The movie's soundtrack, composed by Anand-Milind, became an instant sensation and remains popular to this day[3].

5. The film is often credited with changing the landscape of Indian cinema in the late 1980s and early 1990s, shifting focus back to romantic musicals[4].

6. In 2024, the film celebrated its 36th anniversary, highlighting its enduring popularity and influence on Bollywood[3].

The 1988 release of Qayamat Se Qayamat Tak not only launched the careers of its lead actors but also set a new standard for romantic films in Indian cinema, influencing the industry for years to come.
