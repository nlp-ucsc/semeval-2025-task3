Pico da Neblina is the highest peak in Brazil, with an elevation of 2,995.3 meters (9,827 feet) above sea level[1][4]. This impressive mountain is located in the Serra da Neblina, part of the Serra do Imeri, which is a section of the Guiana Highlands on the Brazil-Venezuela border[1].

Some key facts about Pico da Neblina:

1. Location: It is situated in the state of Amazonas, in the remote Amazon rainforest region of Brazil[2][6].

2. Name meaning: "Pico da Neblina" translates to "Peak of the Mist" in English, reflecting the fact that it is often shrouded in dense clouds[1][5].

3. Discovery: The peak was discovered relatively recently, in the late 1950s, and was first ascended in 1965 by members of a Brazilian Army expedition[1][5].

6. Ecosystem: The area around Pico da Neblina is known for its rich biodiversity, including unique plant species like carnivorous plants and a variety of wildlife[6].

7. Cultural significance: The mountain is located within the territory of the Yanomami indigenous people, who are now involved in sustainable ecotourism projects related to the peak[5][6].
