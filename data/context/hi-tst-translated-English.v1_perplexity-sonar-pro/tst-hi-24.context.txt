Daniel Day-Lewis's spouse is Rebecca Miller[1][2][3]. They met in 1996 when Day-Lewis was working on the film adaptation of Arthur Miller's play "The Crucible," and Rebecca Miller is Arthur Miller's daughter[1][2]. The couple married on November 13, 1996[2][3].

Rebecca Miller is an accomplished filmmaker and novelist in her own right[3]. She has directed several films, including "Angela" (1995), "Personal Velocity: Three Portraits" (2002), "The Ballad of Jack and Rose" (2005), "The Private Lives of Pippa Lee" (2009), and "Maggie's Plan" (2015)[3]. She has also written novels, including "The Private Lives of Pippa Lee" and "Jacob's Folly"[3].

Daniel Day-Lewis and Rebecca Miller have two sons together: Ronan (born 1998) and Cashel (born 2002)[3]. Day-Lewis is also stepfather to Gabriel-Kane Day-Lewis (born 1995), his son from a previous relationship with French actress Isabelle Adjani[1][3].

The couple has been known to be very private about their personal lives[1]. They divide their time between homes in Manhattan and Annamoe, Ireland[2].
