Nutan, the legendary Indian actress, was honored with the Padma Shri in 1974[1][6]. 

Nutan was one of the leading actresses of Indian cinema during the 1950s and 1960s. Her career spanned over 40 years, during which she delivered numerous critically acclaimed performances. She was known for her versatility and her ability to portray complex characters with depth and nuance.

Some key points about Nutan's illustrious career:

1. She won the Filmfare Award for Best Actress five times, a record that stood until her niece Kajol broke it in 2011[1].

2. Some of her most celebrated films include "Seema", "Bandini", "Sujata", and "Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki"[1].

3. Nutan was known for taking on unconventional roles that often addressed social issues[1].

4. She continued to play leading roles well into her 40s, which was rare for actresses of her generation[4].

5. In addition to the Padma Shri, Nutan also received other prestigious awards, including the Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award[1].

The Padma Shri, awarded to Nutan in 1974, is the fourth-highest civilian honor in India. It recognizes distinguished service of a high order in any field, including art. This award was a testament to Nutan's significant contributions to Indian cinema and her impact on the art of acting in India.
