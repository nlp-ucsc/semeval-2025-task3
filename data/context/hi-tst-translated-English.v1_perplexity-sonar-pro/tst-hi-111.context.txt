Chris Colfer gained international recognition for his portrayal of Kurt Hummel on the television musical series Glee, which aired from 2009 to 2015[1][3]. Colfer's performance as Kurt, a fashionable gay countertenor who faces bullying at school, earned him critical acclaim and several awards[1][3].

Some key points about Chris Colfer and his role as Kurt Hummel:

1. Colfer was born on May 27, 1990, in Fresno, California[1][3].

2. He was cast in Glee in 2009, which was his first TV role[1][3].

3. The character of Kurt Hummel was specifically created for Colfer after he impressed the show's creator, Ryan Murphy, during his audition for a different role[1][3].

4. For his portrayal of Kurt, Colfer won the 2011 Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Series, Miniseries or Television Film[1][3].

5. He also received three consecutive People's Choice Awards for Favorite Comedic TV Actor from 2013 to 2015[1].

6. Colfer was nominated twice for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series[1][3].

7. In April 2011, Colfer was named one of the Time 100, Time's list of the 100 most influential people in the world[1].

Colfer's portrayal of Kurt Hummel was groundbreaking for its representation of LGBTQ+ youth on mainstream television. The character's struggles with his sexuality, bullying, and acceptance resonated with many viewers and contributed significantly to Colfer's rise to fame[1][3][4].
