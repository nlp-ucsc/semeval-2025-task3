The 5th president of the Republic of Congo is Denis Sassou Nguesso[8]. He has served as president during two non-consecutive periods:

1. First term: 8 February 1979 - 31 August 1992
2. Second term: 25 October 1997 - Present

Denis Sassou Nguesso is the current incumbent president of the Republic of Congo. He first came to power in 1979 and ruled until 1992. After a period out of office, he returned to the presidency in 1997 following a civil war. He has won subsequent elections in 2002, 2009, 2016, and most recently in 2021[8].

Some key points about Denis Sassou Nguesso's presidency:

- He has been in power for a total of over 38 years across his two terms, making him one of Africa's longest-serving leaders.
- He represents the Congolese Party of Labour (PCT).
- In the latest 2021 election, he won 88.40% of the vote[8].
- The constitution was changed in 2015 to remove presidential age limits and extend term limits, allowing Sassou Nguesso to continue running for office[8].
