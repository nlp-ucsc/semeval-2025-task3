There is indeed a reservation in Gogebic County, Michigan. The Lac Vieux Desert Indian Reservation is located in Watersmeet Township of southeastern Gogebic County, in the western part of Michigan's Upper Peninsula[1]. This reservation is the landbase for the Lac Vieux Desert Band of Lake Superior Chippewa[1].

Key facts about the Lac Vieux Desert Indian Reservation:

1. Location: It is situated in Watersmeet Township, Gogebic County, Michigan[1][2].

2. Size: According to the 2020 United States Census Bureau data, the reservation has a land area of 0.419 square miles (268.16 acres; 1.09 km^2^)[1].

3. Population: As of the 2020 census, there were 221 people living on the reservation[1].

4. Composition: The reservation consists of two sections in eastern Watersmeet Township[1].

5. Tribal affiliation: It is home to the Lac Vieux Desert Band of Lake Superior Chippewa Indians, which is one of twelve bands known as the Lake Superior Bands of Chippewa Indians[2].
