Toruń is located in Poland[1][2][3]. Specifically, it is situated in north-central Poland, on the Vistula River[3][4]. Toruń is part of the Kuyavian-Pomeranian Voivodeship (province) and serves as one of its two capital cities, alongside Bydgoszcz[3][9].

Some key details about Toruń's location and status in Poland:

1. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its well-preserved medieval architecture[2][3].

2. Toruń is one of the oldest cities in Poland, with a history dating back to the 8th century[3].

3. The city's geographical coordinates are approximately 53.013790° N latitude and 18.598444° E longitude[1].

4. As of December 2021, Toruń had a population of 196,935[3].

5. It is an important cultural, economic, and educational center in Poland, home to one of the country's best universities[4].

6. Toruń is renowned for its gingerbread-making tradition, which spans nearly a millennium[3].

7. The city is famous as the birthplace of the astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus, born there in 1473[7].

Toruń's location in Poland has played a significant role in its history, as it has been an important trading center due to its position on the Vistula River and its membership in the Hanseatic League[2][3].
