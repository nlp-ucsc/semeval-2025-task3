Petra van Staveren is known primarily for her outstanding achievement in swimming at the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. She won the gold medal in the women's 100 meter breaststroke event[1][2][3]. This victory was the pinnacle of her swimming career and brought her international recognition.

Some key details about Petra van Staveren:

1. Full name and nationality: Her full name is Petronella Grietje van Staveren, and she is from the Netherlands[2][3].

2. Olympic performance: 
   - Gold medal in the 100 meter breaststroke at the 1984 Los Angeles Olympics[1][2][3]
   - She also competed in the 200 meter breaststroke, finishing 10th[5]

3. Other notable achievements:
   - Bronze medal in the 4x100 meter medley relay at the 1986 World Championships[2][3]
   - Silver medal in the 4x100 meter medley relay at the 1983 European Championships[1][2]

4. Career highlights:
   - She finished fourth place five times at European championships between 1981 and 1985[2][3]
   - Her winning time in the 100 meter breaststroke at the 1984 Olympics was 1:09.88[1]

5. Personal details:
   - Born on June 2, 1966, in Kampen, Overijssel, Netherlands[2][5]
   - She was 18 years old when she won her Olympic gold medal[1]

6. Recognition:
   - She was awarded the title of Knight of the Order of Orange-Nassau, a civil and military Dutch order of chivalry[3]

Petra van Staveren's Olympic gold medal victory in 1984 remains her most significant achievement and the primary reason for her fame in the world of swimming and Dutch sports history.
