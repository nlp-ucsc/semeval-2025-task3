Gregory III Laham belongs to the Melkite Greek Catholic Church. He served as the Patriarch of Antioch and All the East, of Alexandria and Jerusalem for this Eastern Catholic Church from 2000 until his retirement in 2017[1][2].

The Melkite Greek Catholic Church is an Eastern Catholic Church in full communion with the Holy See and the Roman Catholic Church. It is part of the Greek or Byzantine tradition of Eastern Christianity, using Greek liturgical practices, but is distinct from the Eastern Orthodox Church[1][6].

Some key points about Gregory III Laham's religious affiliation:

1. He was elected as the Melkite Greek Catholic Patriarch in November 2000[1].

2. His full title was Patriarch of Antioch and All the East, of Alexandria and Jerusalem of the Melkite Greek Catholic Church[1].

3. He received ecclesiastical communion from Pope John Paul II after his election, confirming the Melkite Church's union with Rome[2].

4. He was a member of the Basilian Salvatorian Order, a religious order within the Melkite Church[1].

5. As Patriarch, he was the spiritual leader of the Melkite Greek Catholic Church, one of the largest Eastern Catholic Churches[2][5].

6. He retired from his position as Patriarch on May 6, 2017, with Pope Francis accepting his resignation[10].
