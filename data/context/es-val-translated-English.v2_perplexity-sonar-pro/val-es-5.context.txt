The Pohang Steelers won the 1997-98 Asian Club Championship[1][4]. This was their second consecutive Asian Champions title[4]. They defeated Dalian Wanda in the final match, winning 6-5 on penalties after the game ended in a 0-0 draw[1][4].

Some key details about Pohang Steelers' victory:

1. The final was held in Hong Kong in April 1998[1][4].

2. Pohang Steelers advanced to the final by topping their group in the East Asia section and then defeating Al-Hilal 1-0 in the semi-final[2][4].

3. In the group stage, Pohang Steelers finished first with 6 points from 3 matches, scoring 8 goals and conceding 4[1][2].

4. This victory marked Pohang Steelers' second Asian Club Championship title, having also won the previous year's competition (1996-97)[3][4].

5. The tournament featured 29 teams from across Asia and consisted of 58 matches in total[4].

Pohang Steelers' success in this tournament solidified their status as one of the strongest clubs in Asian football during that period. Their back-to-back victories in 1996-97 and 1997-98 were part of a successful era for South Korean clubs in the Asian Club Championship[3].
