Yusra Mardini was a member of the Refugee Olympic Team at the 2016 Summer Olympics in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The Refugee Olympic Team, officially known as the "Team of Refugee Olympic Athletes," was a group of athletes who were unable to compete for their home countries due to displacement. Mardini, a Syrian swimmer, competed in the women's 100-meter freestyle and 100-meter butterfly events. Her story gained international attention as she had fled Syria and faced significant challenges before reaching safety and continuing her athletic career.
