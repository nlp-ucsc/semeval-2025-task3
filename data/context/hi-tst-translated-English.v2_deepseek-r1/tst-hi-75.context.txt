Traditionalism, as a distinct intellectual and cultural movement, primarily developed in the **19th century**. This was a response to the Enlightenment, the French Revolution, and the rise of modernity. Thinkers like Joseph de Maistre, Louis de Bonald, and later Émile Durkheim in sociology, played key roles in shaping traditionalist thought.

In the **20th century**, traditionalism continued to evolve, influencing various fields such as philosophy, anthropology, and religion. The Perennialist school, led by figures like René Guénon and Frithjof Schuon, is an example of this evolution.

So, while traditionalism has older roots, its most significant development occurred in the **19th century**.
