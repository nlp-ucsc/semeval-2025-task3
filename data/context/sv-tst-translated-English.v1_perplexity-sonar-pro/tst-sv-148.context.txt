The Leftovers ran for 3 seasons on HBO[1][2][3]. 

The series premiered on June 29, 2014 and concluded on June 4, 2017[2]. Here's a breakdown of the seasons:

1. Season 1: Consisted of 10 episodes and aired from June 29 to September 7, 2014[2].

2. Season 2: Also had 10 episodes and aired from October 4 to December 6, 2015[1].

3. Season 3: The final season had a shortened run of 8 episodes and aired from April 16 to June 4, 2017[1][2].

In total, 28 episodes of The Leftovers were produced across its three-season run[1][2]. The show was created by Damon Lindelof and Tom Perrotta, based on Perrotta's novel of the same name. It starred an ensemble cast including Justin Theroux, Amy Brenneman, Christopher Eccleston, Liv Tyler, and Carrie Coon[1].

The series received critical acclaim, particularly for its second and third seasons, with many critics considering it one of the greatest television series of all time[1]. Despite its critical success, the show maintained average Nielsen ratings throughout its run but developed a strong cult following[1].
