Laurance Rockefeller served as chairman of the Rockefeller Brothers Fund (RBF) from 1968 to 1980[1][7]. Prior to his role as chairman, he was also the president of the RBF from 1958 to 1968[1]. His tenure as chairman of the RBF, lasting 22 years in total, was longer than any other leader in the Fund's history[1].

The Rockefeller Brothers Fund is a philanthropic organization founded by the Rockefeller brothers in 1940. Laurance Rockefeller was one of the founding trustees and played a significant role in shaping the fund's direction and activities[1]. During his leadership, the RBF was involved in various philanthropic endeavors, including support for conservation efforts, civil rights initiatives, and international development projects[7].
