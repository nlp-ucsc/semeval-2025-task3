Gergely Kulcsár won Olympic medals in javelin throw, which is a track and field athletics event[1][4]. Specifically:

1. He won a silver medal in javelin throw at the 1964 Tokyo Olympics[1][4].

2. He won bronze medals in javelin throw at two Olympics:
   - The 1960 Rome Olympics
   - The 1968 Mexico City Olympics[1][4]

Kulcsár was a highly accomplished javelin thrower who competed in four Olympic Games from 1960 to 1972[1]. He was the first Hungarian to throw the javelin over 80 meters and won 12 national titles in the event[1][3]. In addition to his Olympic success, Kulcsár also won bronze medals at the European Championships in 1958 and 1966[3].

His personal best throw was 87.06 meters, achieved in 1968[1]. After retiring as an athlete, Kulcsár had a successful coaching career, guiding Miklós Németh to Olympic gold in javelin throw in 1976[3][7].
