John Christopher Willis (1868-1958) lived in several places throughout his lifetime:

1. Liverpool, England: Willis was born in Birkenhead, near Liverpool, on February 20, 1868[1][5].

2. Cambridge, England: He studied botany at Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge[1].

3. Ceylon (now Sri Lanka): In 1896, Willis was appointed director of the Royal Botanical Gardens in Peradeniya, Ceylon, where he worked until 1912[1][5].

4. Rio de Janeiro, Brazil: From 1912 to 1915, he served as the director of the botanic gardens in Rio de Janeiro[1][3][5].

5. Cambridge, England (again): After his retirement from Rio de Janeiro, Willis returned to Cambridge to continue his work[1][3].

6. Montreux, Switzerland: In his later years, Willis moved to Montreux, Switzerland. He lived at Les Terrages, Avenue des Alpes, where he spent the final years of his life[3][5].

Willis died in Montreux on March 21, 1958, at the age of 90[1][5]. Throughout his career, he traveled extensively for his botanical research, but these locations represent the primary places where he resided for extended periods.
