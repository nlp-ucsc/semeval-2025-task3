Kaspiysk is a city located in the Republic of Dagestan, Russia[1][2]. It is situated on the western shore of the Caspian Sea, approximately 18 kilometers (11 miles) southeast of Makhachkala, the capital city of Dagestan[1][2].

Some key points about Kaspiysk's location:

1. Geographical coordinates: The city is located at 42°53'17.309" N 47°38'32.883" E[1].

2. Coastal position: Kaspiysk is a coastal city, directly bordering the Caspian Sea, which is actually the world's largest inland body of water[2][3].

3. Regional context: It is part of the North Caucasus region of Russia, in the southernmost part of the country[6].

4. Proximity to other cities: Besides being close to Makhachkala, Kaspiysk is also near other Dagestani cities like Derbent, Kizlyar, and Izberbash[6].

5. Transportation: The city has its own railway station connecting it to other major cities in the region[3]. It's also conveniently close to Makhachkala airport, which is actually nearer to Kaspiysk than to Makhachkala itself[3].

Kaspiysk is a relatively young city, founded in 1932 as a workers' settlement for a nearby naval diesel engine manufacturer[2]. Its strategic location on the Caspian Sea has made it an important center for naval and military industries in Russia[2][3].
