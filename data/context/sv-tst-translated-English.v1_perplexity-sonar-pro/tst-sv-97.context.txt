Tom Hanks played the lead role of Robert Langdon in the film adaptation of Dan Brown's book "Inferno"[1][2][5]. This was Hanks' third time portraying the character of Robert Langdon, following his performances in "The Da Vinci Code" (2006) and "Angels & Demons" (2009)[4]. 

The film was directed by Ron Howard and released in 2016[1][5]. It's based on Dan Brown's 2013 novel of the same name and is the third installment in the Robert Langdon film series[5].

In "Inferno", Hanks' character Robert Langdon wakes up in an Italian hospital with amnesia and teams up with Dr. Sienna Brooks, played by Felicity Jones, to race across Europe against time to stop a deadly global plot[1][2]. The film also features other notable actors such as Irrfan Khan, Omar Sy, Ben Foster, and Sidse Babett Knudsen in supporting roles[5].
