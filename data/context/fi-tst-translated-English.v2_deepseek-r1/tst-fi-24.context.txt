The nation that arrived in the Mapungubwe area around the 10th century was the ancestors of the modern-day Tsonga, Karanga, and Kalanga people. By the 13th century, Mapungubwe became the capital of a powerful and prosperous kingdom known as the Kingdom of Mapungubwe. This kingdom was part of the broader cultural and trading networks of the Shona people. The Mapungubwe culture is known for its advanced metallurgy, trade networks, and well-organized society.
