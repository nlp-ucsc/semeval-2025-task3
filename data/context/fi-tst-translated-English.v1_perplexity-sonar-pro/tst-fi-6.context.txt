Barcelona won the 2011 UEFA Super Cup, defeating Porto 2-0 in the final[1][3][8].

The match took place on August 26, 2011 at the Stade Louis II in Monaco[1]. Barcelona qualified for the Super Cup as the winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Champions League, while Porto qualified as the winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Europa League[1].

The goals for Barcelona were scored by:

1. Lionel Messi in the 39th minute
2. Cesc Fàbregas in the 88th minute[3][8]

This victory marked Barcelona's fourth UEFA Super Cup title, further cementing their status as one of Europe's most successful clubs[8]. The win came during Josep Guardiola's tenure as manager, adding to the impressive list of trophies the team won under his leadership[8].

Some additional details about the match:

- Andrés Iniesta of Barcelona was named Man of the Match[1]
- The referee was Björn Kuipers from the Netherlands[1]
- The attendance was 18,048[1]
- Weather conditions were clear, with a temperature of 27°C (81°F) and 67% humidity[1]

This victory continued Barcelona's strong performance in European competitions during this period, showcasing the talent of players like Messi, Iniesta, and Fàbregas.
