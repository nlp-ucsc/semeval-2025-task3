The province south of Shanghai known for its cuisine is Zhejiang. Zhejiang is famous for its Zhe cuisine, which is one of the eight major cuisines in China. It's known for its fresh flavors, emphasis on seafood, and use of local ingredients. Popular dishes include Dongpo pork, beggar's chicken, and steamed fish with a wine sauce. Zhejiang's capital, Hangzhou, is particularly renowned for its culinary offerings, such as the well-known dish "xi hu (West Lake) copper pot sticky rice." The province's cuisine is celebrated for its balance of flavors and emphasis on seasonality.
