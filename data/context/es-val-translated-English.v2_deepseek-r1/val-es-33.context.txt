The real name of the rapper known as Chance the Rapper is Chancelor Johnathan Bennett. He was born on April 16, 1993, in Chicago, Illinois. Chance the Rapper gained widespread recognition for his unique style, which often incorporates elements of hip-hop, jazz, and gospel music. He first came to prominence with his 2013 mixtape "Acid Rap," and has since released several critically acclaimed projects, including "Coloring Book" in 2016, which earned him a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album in 2017. known for his introspective and often spiritually-themed lyrics, Chance the Rapper has become a prominent figure in contemporary music.
