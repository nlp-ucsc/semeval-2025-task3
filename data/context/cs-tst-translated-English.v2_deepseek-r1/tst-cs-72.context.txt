The group of painters founded in 1918 that included Josef Čapek, Václav Špála, and Jan Zrzavý was known as the "Group of Fine Artists." This group was established in Prague and played a significant role in promoting modern art in Czechoslovakia during the early 20th century. The collective aimed to foster a sense of community among artists and to organize exhibitions that showcased their work, contributing to the development of modern art movements in the region. The group's formation was an important milestone in Czech art history, as it brought together influential artists who shared a commitment to innovation and artistic expression.
