The Libice chateau, also known as Libice Castle, belongs to the **Gothic architectural style**, which was prevalent in Europe during the medieval period. Libice Castle, located in the Czech Republic, was initially constructed in the 12th century, and its design reflects the characteristic features of Gothic architecture, such as pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and tall, imposing structures. Over the centuries, it has undergone various renovations and modifications, but its primary architectural style remains rooted in the Gothic tradition.
