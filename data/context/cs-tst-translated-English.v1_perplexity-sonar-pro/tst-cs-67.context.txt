The faculty of the Academy of Performing Arts in Prague focused on theater disciplines uses the abbreviation DAMU[1][2][5]. 

DAMU stands for "Divadelní fakulta Akademie múzických umění v Praze" in Czech, which translates to "Theatre Faculty of the Academy of Performing Arts in Prague" in English[5]. This faculty is one of the three main components of the Academy of Performing Arts in Prague (AMU), alongside the Film and TV School (FAMU) and the Music and Dance Faculty (HAMU)[1].

DAMU was established in 1945 as part of the newly created Academy of Performing Arts in Prague[5]. It is a modern, university-level art school that provides professional education in various branches of theater, including acting, directing, dramaturgy, scenography, theory and criticism, theater management, authorial creativity, and drama in education[2].

The faculty offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral level studies[2]. DAMU is known for its selective admission process and its strong connections with the professional theater world, with many prominent personalities of Czech theatrical life teaching at the school[2][5].
