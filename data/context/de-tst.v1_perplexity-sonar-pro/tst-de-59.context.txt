Richard V. Spencer served as the 76th United States Secretary of the Navy from August 3, 2017 to November 24, 2019[1][5][9]. 

Some key details about Spencer's tenure as Secretary of the Navy include:

- He was nominated by President Donald Trump in June 2017 and confirmed by the Senate on August 1, 2017[5].

- He was sworn in as Secretary of the Navy on August 3, 2017[1][9].

- Spencer briefly served as Acting Secretary of Defense from July 15-23, 2019 and Acting Deputy Secretary of Defense from July 24-31, 2019 while continuing his role as Navy Secretary[5].

- His term ended on November 24, 2019 when he was asked to resign by Secretary of Defense Mark Esper over his handling of the Eddie Gallagher case[5].

Prior to becoming Secretary of the Navy, Spencer had served as a U.S. Marine Corps aviator from 1976-1981[1][5]. He was born on January 18, 1954 in Waterbury, Connecticut and graduated from Rollins College in 1976 with a degree in economics[1][7]. After his military service, Spencer had a career in finance, including serving as Vice Chairman and CFO of Intercontinental Exchange from 2001-2008[5].
