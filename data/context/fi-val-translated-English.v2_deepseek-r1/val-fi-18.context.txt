Johnny Cash served in the United States Air Force when the Korean War broke out. He enlisted in 1950 and was stationed in West Germany, where he was a Morse code intercept operator. His service in the Air Force had a significant impact on his life and career, as it was during this time that he began writing songs, including some of his most famous ones like "Folsom Prison Blues." After his honorable discharge in 1954 with the rank of Staff Sergeant, Cash went on to pursue a career in music, becoming one of the most iconic figures in country and American roots music.
