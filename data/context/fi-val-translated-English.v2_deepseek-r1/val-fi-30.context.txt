In Katsina, Nigeria, the largest population groups are the Hausa and Fulani people. The Hausa are the predominant ethnic group in the state, known for their rich cultural heritage and dominance in trade and commerce. The Fulani, on the other hand, are primarily pastoralists and also form a significant part of the population. Both groups are predominantly Muslim, contributing to the Islamic majority in the region. Katsina's population is also influenced by various other ethnic groups, but Hausa and Fulani remain the most populous.
