John Swinney is a member of the Scottish National Party (SNP)[1][5]. He has been a prominent figure in the SNP for many years:

- He joined the SNP in 1979 at the age of 15[1].
- He served as the SNP's National Secretary from 1986 to 1992[1].
- He was Deputy Leader of the SNP from 1998 to 2000[1].
- He was elected Leader of the SNP in 2000 and served in that role until 2004[1].
- In 2024, he was elected unopposed as leader of the SNP for a second time[1][2].
- He currently serves as First Minister of Scotland and leader of the SNP[1][5].

Throughout his political career, Swinney has held various high-ranking positions within the SNP and Scottish government, including:

- Member of Parliament (MP) for Tayside North from 1997 to 2001[1]
- Member of the Scottish Parliament (MSP) since 1999[1][4]
- Deputy First Minister of Scotland from 2014 to 2023[1]
- First Minister of Scotland since 2024[1][5]

Swinney has been a key figure in the SNP's push for Scottish independence and has played a significant role in shaping the party's policies and direction over the past few decades[1][5].
