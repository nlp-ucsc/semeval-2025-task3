Chuseok, also known as Korean Thanksgiving Day, is a major traditional holiday in Korea that celebrates the harvest season. It is a time when families gather together to honor their ancestors and give thanks for the year's bounty. The celebration typically involves traditional foods such as songpyeon (half-moon-shaped rice cakes), visiting ancestral graves to pay respects, and various cultural performances and games. It is a significant holiday that reflects Korean values of family, gratitude, and respect for one's heritage.
