According to the Nordic sagas, the fortress of Jomsborg was located in present-day Poland, near the mouth of the Oder River. Specifically, it is associated with the area around the town of Jomsborg, which is now known as Jutrzenka or Wolin, located on Wolin Island in the Baltic Sea. Jomsborg was a legendary fortress said to be the stronghold of the Jomsvikings, a group of Viking warriors known for their strict code of conduct and their prowess in battle. The sagas describe Jomsborg as a formidable fortress, strategically positioned to control the trade and naval routes in the Baltic region.
