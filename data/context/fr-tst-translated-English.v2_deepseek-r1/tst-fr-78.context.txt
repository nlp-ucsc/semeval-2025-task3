Laurance Rockefeller purchased a significant portion of the island of Saint John in the U.S. Virgin Islands in 1954. With this acquisition, he developed the area into the exclusive resort known as the Rockefeller. His vision for the resort was to create a luxury destination that would attract high-profile guests while preserving the natural beauty of the island. Over time, the resort became a symbol of elegance and exclusivity, offering a unique Caribbean experience to its visitors. Rockefeller's investment in Saint John not only transformed the island's economy but also set a precedent for sustainable and luxurious tourism in the region.
