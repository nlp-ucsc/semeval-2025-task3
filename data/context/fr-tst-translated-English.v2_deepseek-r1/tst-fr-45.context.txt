The village is renowned for its prize-winning cheese, "Maroilles," which is a type of washed-rind cheese known for its strong aroma and golden-orange color. The name "Maroilles" has historical roots, possibly dating back to the Middle Ages, and is associated with the local dialect and the rich agricultural traditions of the area.
