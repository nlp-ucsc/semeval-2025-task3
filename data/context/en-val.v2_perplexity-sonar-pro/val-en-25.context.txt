The municipalities of Gampel and Bratsch merged on January 1, 2009 to form the new municipality of Gampel-Bratsch[2]. This merger was the result of a vote held on January 20, 2008, where the residents of both municipalities decided to combine into a single administrative entity[2][4].

Some key points about this merger:

1. The decision to merge was made by the voters of both Gampel and Bratsch on January 20, 2008[2].

2. The actual merger took effect on January 1, 2009, creating the new municipality of Gampel-Bratsch[2][4].

3. Prior to the merger, Gampel and Bratsch were independent municipalities in the district of Leuk in the canton of Valais, Switzerland[4].

4. This merger is part of a broader trend in Switzerland where smaller municipalities sometimes combine to improve administrative efficiency and resource allocation.

5. After the merger, the new municipality of Gampel-Bratsch continued to be located in the district of Leuk in the canton of Valais[2].

This merger has had ongoing effects on the community. For instance, as of 2024, the municipality is undertaking a project to rename several streets, changing them from local dialect to standard German, which will affect many residential addresses[3].
