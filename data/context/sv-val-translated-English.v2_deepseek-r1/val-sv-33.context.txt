The Kremlin is a fortified complex in the heart of Moscow, Russia, and it is the oldest and most famous kremlin in the country. The history of the Moscow Kremlin dates back to 1147, making it over 875 years old. However, the current walls and structures of the Kremlin were built in the 15th century. The term "kremlin" is also used to refer to other fortified complexes in Russia, such as those in cities like Kazan, Nizhny Novgorod, and others, each with its own specific age and historical significance.

Therefore, the oldest kremlin in Russia is the Moscow Kremlin, which has a history dating back to the 12th century, with its current form constructed in the 15th century.
