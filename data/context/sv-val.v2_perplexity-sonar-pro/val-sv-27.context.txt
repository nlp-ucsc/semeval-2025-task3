Jef Raskin died at the age of 61[1][2][4]. He was born on March 9, 1943, and passed away on February 26, 2005, in Pacifica, California[1][4]. Raskin was just shy of his 62nd birthday when he died, as he passed away about two weeks before he would have turned 62.

Raskin was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in December 2004, only a few months before his death[1][4]. Despite his illness, he continued working on his projects, including the Archy software, until the last days of his life[1].

Jef Raskin was a pioneering figure in the field of human-computer interaction. He is best known for initiating the Macintosh project at Apple Computer in 1979[2][4]. He joined Apple in 1978 as employee number 31 and played a crucial role in developing the concept of an affordable, easy-to-use computer for consumers[2][4].
