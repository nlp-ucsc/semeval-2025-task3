Carl Axel Löwenhielm had several prominent careers and roles throughout his life:

1. Military Officer: Löwenhielm had a distinguished military career, rising through the ranks[1][4]:
   - He participated in war operations in Finland (1788-1790), Pomerania (1807), and on the Norwegian border and Åland (1809)[1].
   - Promoted to Lieutenant Colonel in 1808[1].
   - Became General Adjuster in 1812[1].
   - Promoted to Major General in 1814[1][4].
   - Attained the rank of Lieutenant-General in 1815[1][4].

2. Diplomat: Löwenhielm served in several diplomatic capacities[1][4]:
   - Swedish envoy to St. Petersburg from 1812 to 1818[4].
   - Represented Sweden at the Congress of Vienna in 1814-1815[1][7].
   - Negotiated an important agreement with Russia in 1812, securing Russian support for Sweden's acquisition of Norway[4].

3. Politician: He held significant political positions[1][4]:
   - Member of the Swedish cabinet from 1822 to 1839[1][4].
   - Served as President of the War College (Krigskollegium) from 1819[4].

4. Court Official: Löwenhielm held various positions at the Swedish royal court[4]:
   - Became a chamberlain to Queen Frederica in 1797[4].
   - Appointed cabinet chamberlain to Crown Prince Charles John (later King Charles XIV John) in 1810[4].
   - Served as överstekammarjunkare (First Gentleman of the Bedchamber) from 1816[4].

5. Administrator: He took on administrative roles later in life[4]:
   - Chairman of the Board of Prisons and Labor Institutions in the Kingdom (Styrelsen över fängelser och arbetsinrättningar i riket) from 1825 to 1826[4].
   - Chairman of the Table Commission (precursor to the Central Bureau of Statistics) from 1838 until his death[4].

These diverse roles demonstrate Carl Axel Löwenhielm's significant contributions to Swedish military, diplomatic, political, and administrative affairs throughout his long career.
