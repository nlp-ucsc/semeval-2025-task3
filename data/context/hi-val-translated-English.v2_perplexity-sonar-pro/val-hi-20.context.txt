The International Chess Federation (FIDE) was established 100 years ago, in 1924[1][2][4]. Specifically, FIDE was founded on July 20, 1924, in Paris, France[2][4][8].

Some key points about FIDE's founding and history:

1. FIDE was one of the first international sports federations, alongside those for football, cricket, swimming, and auto racing[8].

2. The organization was initially referred to as FIE (Fédération Internationale des Echecs) and adopted the acronym FIDE in 1925 at the request of the Italian delegation[9].

3. FIDE's motto is "Gens una sumus," which is Latin for "We are one family"[2][4].

4. The founding countries included Argentina, Belgium, Britain, Hungary, Spain, Italy, Canada, the Netherlands, Poland, Romania, Finland, France, Czechoslovakia, Switzerland, and Yugoslavia[1].

5. Pierre Vincent, the Secretary General of the French Chess Federation, is regarded as the founder of FIDE[9].

6. FIDE began overseeing the World Chess Championship in 1948[1].

7. In 1999, FIDE was recognized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) as the supreme body responsible for chess[1][4].

As of 2024, FIDE is celebrating its centenary anniversary with various events and initiatives, including a Chess Torch Relay and special celebrations at the Chess Olympiad in Budapest[10].
