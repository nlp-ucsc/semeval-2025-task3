Posey County is located in the state of Indiana[1][2][3]. It is the southernmost, southwesternmost, and westernmost county in Indiana[1][2]. The county's southern border is formed by the Ohio River, while its western border is formed by the Wabash River, a tributary of the Ohio[1][2].

Some key facts about Posey County's location:

1. It is part of the Evansville, Indiana metropolitan statistical area[1][2].

2. The county seat is Mount Vernon[1][2][3].

3. As of 2020, the population of Posey County was 25,222[1][2][3].

4. The county has a total area of 419.32 square miles (1,086.0 km²), of which 409.57 square miles (1,060.8 km²) is land and 9.75 square miles (25.3 km²) is water[1][2].

5. Posey County is home to the lowest point in the state of Indiana, located where the Wabash River flows into the Ohio River[1][2].

6. The county is bordered by Gibson County to the north and northeast, Vanderburgh County to the east, Henderson County (Kentucky) to the southeast, Union County (Kentucky) to the south, Gallatin County (Illinois) to the southwest, and White County (Illinois) to the west and northwest[2].

Posey County's unique location at the southwestern tip of Indiana gives it a distinctive geographical and historical significance within the state.
