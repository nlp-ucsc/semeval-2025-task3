The fictional character Elaka Måns was created by the Swedish author and playwright August Strindberg. Elaka Måns, which translates to "Evil Måns" or "Wicked Måns," is a character from Strindberg's play "Måns och Brita" (Måns and Brita), written in 1902. The play is a historical drama set in the 17th century and revolves around the doomed love between Måns, a nobleman, and Brita, a lower-class woman. The character of Elaka Måns is notable for his complexity and the darker aspects of his personality, which reflect Strindberg's exploration of human nature and moral ambiguity in his works.
