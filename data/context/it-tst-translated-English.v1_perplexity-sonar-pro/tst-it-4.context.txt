Dioumaténé is a village and rural commune located in the Cercle of Kadiolo in the Sikasso Region of southern Mali[1][2]. Specifically:

- It is part of the Sikasso Region, which is one of Mali's administrative regions.
- Within the Sikasso Region, Dioumaténé belongs to the Cercle of Kadiolo.

The commune of Dioumaténé covers an area of 225 square kilometers and includes 5 villages. The village of Dioumaténé itself serves as the administrative center (chef-lieu) of the commune and is situated 16 km west of Kadiolo[1][2].

According to the 2009 census, the commune of Dioumaténé had a population of 7,863 inhabitants[4]. This population was distributed as follows:

- Males: 3,745
- Females: 4,118

It's worth noting that the commune system in Mali is part of the country's decentralized administrative structure. Communes are the third-level administrative units in Mali, below regions and cercles[8]. They are similar to municipalities in other countries and are governed by elected officials, including a mayor and a commune council[8].
