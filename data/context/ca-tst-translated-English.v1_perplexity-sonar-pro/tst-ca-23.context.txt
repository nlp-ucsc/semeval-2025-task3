The current Prime Minister of Togo is Victoire Sidémého Tomegah-Dogbé[1][5]. She has held this position since September 28, 2020, making her the first woman to serve as Prime Minister of Togo[5].

Some key points about Prime Minister Tomegah-Dogbé:

1. She was reappointed as Prime Minister on August 1, 2024, under Togo's new constitution, which transformed the country from a presidential system to a parliamentary one[1][3].

2. Under the new constitutional arrangement, her role has been strengthened. The position of Prime Minister has been renamed to "President of the Council of Ministers" (Président du Conseil des Ministres in French), and it is now the most powerful position in the country's government[5].

3. Her current term is set for six years under the new constitution[1].

4. Prior to becoming Prime Minister, she held several ministerial positions, including Minister of Grassroots Development, Handicrafts, Youth and Youth Employment, and served as the Cabinet Director for President Faure Gnassingbé[5][9].

5. Tomegah-Dogbé has a strong educational background, holding a master's degree in Economics and Management from the University of Benin-Lomé and a Master's degree in Governance and Development from Jones International University in New York[6].

6. She is married and is the mother of four daughters[6].

Prime Minister Tomegah-Dogbé's appointment and subsequent reappointment represent significant milestones in Togo's political landscape, particularly in terms of women's leadership and constitutional changes in the country's governance structure.
