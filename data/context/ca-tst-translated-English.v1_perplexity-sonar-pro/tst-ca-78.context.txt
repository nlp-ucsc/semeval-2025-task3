Gu Bon-gil is a South Korean sabre fencer[1][2]. He specializes in the sabre discipline of fencing, competing at the highest levels of international competition. Gu is a highly accomplished athlete in this sport, with numerous achievements including:

1. Olympic medals: He is a three-time Olympic gold medalist in the team sabre event, winning at the 2012 London Olympics, 2020 Tokyo Olympics (held in 2021), and 2024 Paris Olympics[1][2].

2. World Championships: Gu has won multiple medals at the World Fencing Championships, including four gold medals in the team sabre event (2017, 2018, 2019, and 2022)[1].

3. Asian Games: He has won multiple gold medals at the Asian Games, including individual sabre gold in 2010, 2014, and 2018[1][7].

4. World Cup and Grand Prix: Gu has numerous victories and podium finishes in World Cup and Grand Prix events throughout his career[1].

Gu began fencing in 2002 in Daegu, South Korea, after being encouraged to take up the sport by a fencing coach at his middle school[3]. He is known for his aggressive fencing style, describing it as "80% attack and 20% defence"[3]. His skill and achievements have earned him recognition in South Korea, including induction into the International Fencing Federation (FIE) Hall of Fame in 2020 and receiving the Cheongryong Medal, the highest Order of Sport Merit in South Korea, in 2019[3].
