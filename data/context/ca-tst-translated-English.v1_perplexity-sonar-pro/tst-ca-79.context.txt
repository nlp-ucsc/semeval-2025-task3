Iuri Gazinski (also spelled Yuri Gazinski) is a professional football (soccer) player[1][2]. He plays as a midfielder, primarily in the center midfield position, though he can also play as a defensive or attacking midfielder[2].

Some key details about Gazinski's football career:

1. Current club: As of the 2024/25 season, Gazinski is playing for FK Krasnodar in the Russian Premier League[1][3].

2. National team: He has represented the Russian national football team, earning 21 FIFA-recognized appearances and scoring 1 goal[2].

3. Club history: Gazinski has played for several Russian clubs throughout his career, including Ural Yekaterinburg, FK Krasnodar, Torpedo Moskva, and Luch-Energia Vladivostok[1].

4. Notable achievements: He was part of the Russian national team that competed in the 2018 FIFA World Cup, where he scored a goal in the opening match against Saudi Arabia[1][2].

5. Playing style: As a central midfielder, Gazinski is known for his versatility, being able to contribute both defensively and offensively in the midfield area.

Gazinski has had a long career in Russian football, playing at various levels of the country's football pyramid, from the third tier (III) up to the top-flight Russian Premier League (I)[1].
