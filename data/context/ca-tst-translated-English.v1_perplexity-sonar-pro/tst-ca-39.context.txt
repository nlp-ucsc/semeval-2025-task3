Saint-Clément is a commune located in the Yonne department in the region of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté in north-central France[3]. 

Some key details about Saint-Clément:

- It is situated in the Yonne department, which is part of the larger Bourgogne-Franche-Comté administrative region[1][3].
- The commune has a population of around 2,800 residents as of recent estimates[7].
- Its geographical coordinates are 48°13′07″N 3°17′37″E[1][3].
- The total area of the commune is approximately 8.47 square kilometers[2].
- Saint-Clément is part of the Communauté d'agglomération du Grand Sénonais, an intercommunal structure centered on the city of Sens[4].

The Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region where Saint-Clément is located was formed in 2016 from the merger of the former regions of Burgundy and Franche-Comté. This region is known for its rich history, wine production, and diverse landscapes ranging from rolling hills to mountain ranges.
