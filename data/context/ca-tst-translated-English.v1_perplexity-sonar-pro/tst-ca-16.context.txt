The protagonist of Dan Brown's novel "Inferno" is Robert Langdon[1][3][5][8]. Robert Langdon is a fictional character created by Dan Brown who appears in several of his novels, including "Angels & Demons," "The Da Vinci Code," "The Lost Symbol," and "Inferno"[1][8].

Robert Langdon is described as a Harvard University professor of Religious Iconology and Symbology[8]. He is known for his brilliant problem-solving mind, eidetic memory, and understanding of semiotics[8]. In "Inferno," Langdon wakes up in an Italian hospital with no memory of recent events and finds himself embroiled in a plot involving a deadly virus and clues related to Dante Alighieri's epic poem "The Divine Comedy"[1][3].

Some key characteristics of Robert Langdon include:

1. He was born on June 22 in Exeter, New Hampshire[1][8].
2. He attended Phillips Exeter Academy and later Princeton University[8].
3. He suffers from claustrophobia due to falling into a well as a child[8].
4. He is often described wearing a turtleneck, Harris Tweed jacket, khakis, and cordovan loafers[8].
5. He has a distinctive Mickey Mouse watch, a gift from his parents[8].

In the film adaptations of Dan Brown's novels, including "Inferno," Robert Langdon is portrayed by actor Tom Hanks[8][9].
