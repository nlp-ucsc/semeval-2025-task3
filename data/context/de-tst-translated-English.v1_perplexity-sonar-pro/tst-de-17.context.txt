The 2011 UEFA Super Cup was contested between Barcelona and FC Porto[1][2]. 

Barcelona qualified for the Super Cup as the winners of the 2010-11 UEFA Champions League, while Porto earned their place by winning the 2010-11 UEFA Europa League[3].

The match was played on August 26, 2011 at the Stade Louis II in Monaco[1]. Barcelona won the game 2-0, with goals from Lionel Messi in the 39th minute and Cesc Fàbregas in the 87th minute[1][2]. 

This victory gave Barcelona their fourth UEFA Super Cup title[9], cementing their status as one of the most successful clubs in the competition's history. For Barcelona, it was their record 8th appearance in the Super Cup[9].

Key players for Barcelona included Lionel Messi, Xavi, Andrés Iniesta, and David Villa, while Porto featured players like Hulk, João Moutinho, and Rolando[1][5].

The match was notable for two red cards given to Porto players - Rolando in the 86th minute and Fredy Guarín in the 90th minute[1].
