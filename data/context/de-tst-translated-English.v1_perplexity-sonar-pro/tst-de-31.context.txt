John Swinney studied Politics at the University of Edinburgh, graduating with a Master of Arts (MA) with Honours in Politics[1][3][4]. He completed his degree in 1986[3].

Some key details about Swinney's education:

- He attended Forrester High School in Edinburgh before going to university[3][6].
- He joined the Scottish National Party (SNP) in 1979 at age 15, while still in high school[1][4].
- After graduating from the University of Edinburgh in 1986, he began his professional career, working in various roles before entering politics full-time[1][3].

Swinney's educational background in politics likely helped prepare him for his long career in Scottish politics, including his roles as SNP leader, Deputy First Minister, and now First Minister of Scotland[1][2][4].
