Alan Rachins was born on October 3, 1942 in Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA[1][2][3]. He was the only child of Edward and Ida Rachins of Brookline, Massachusetts[2]. Both his parents were Jewish, and his father worked in a family food manufacturing business called Snow Crest[2].

Rachins grew up in the Boston area and graduated from Brookline High School in 1960[2][5]. After high school, he initially enrolled at the Wharton School of Finance and Commerce at the University of Pennsylvania, planning to enter the family business[2]. However, he ultimately dropped out and moved to New York to pursue acting[2].

In New York, Rachins studied acting with teachers including William Ball, Warren Robertson, Kim Stanley, and Harvey Lembeck[7]. He later graduated from Empire State College in 1974[2].

Rachins began his acting career on stage in the late 1960s, appearing in Broadway and off-Broadway productions[1][2]. He later transitioned to television and film roles, becoming best known for playing Douglas Brackman Jr. on the series "L.A. Law" from 1986 to 1994 and Larry Finkelstein on "Dharma & Greg" from 1997 to 2002[1][2][3].
